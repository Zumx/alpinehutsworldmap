Austria built the modern alpine hut. The Austrian and German Alpine Clubs
between them maintain hundreds of *Hütten* across the Tyrol, Salzburg, and
Carinthia, linked by waymarked trails that make multi-day traverses almost
casual. These ten are the ones worth structuring a trip around.

## 1. Olpererhütte, Zillertal

Famous for one thing: a slender footbridge over a turquoise reservoir with the
hut behind it. The walk in from Schlegeisspeicher is short, which is why the
bridge is rarely empty.

## 2. Stüdlhütte, Grossglockner

The launch pad for Austria's highest summit. A modern, energy-efficient
building at 2,800 m that fills with climbers checking crampons by headlamp.

## 3. Berliner Hütte, Zillertal

A protected historic monument — more grand hotel than mountain shelter, with a
chandeliered dining room that feels improbable at 2,000 m.

## 4. Adlersruhe (Erzherzog-Johann-Hütte)

The highest hut in Austria at 3,454 m, sitting on the Grossglockner's shoulder.
Sleeping here is itself an altitude experience.

## 5. Hesshütte, Gesäuse

Deep in the limestone walls of the Gesäuse National Park, a base for some of the
country's most serious rock routes and a quiet valley if you only want to walk.

## 6. Glorer Hütte, Hohe Tauern

A small, friendly hut on a panoramic saddle, ideal as a gentle first overnight
with the Grossglockner filling the horizon.

## 7. Franz-Senn-Hütte, Stubai

A large, family-run institution and one of the best bases for the Stubai
glacier circuit, with a dedicated children's programme in summer.

## 8. Karlsbader Hütte, Lienz Dolomites

Sat beside a small lake under the dramatic Laserz towers — arguably the most
photogenic setting in the eastern Alps.

## 9. Edelhütte, Zillertal

The oldest hut in the Zillertal, recently modernised, with a balcony walk that
opens onto the Ahornspitze.

## 10. Hochjoch-Hospiz, Ötztal

A historic glacier hut with a long mountaineering pedigree on the route toward
the Weisskugel.

## The Alpenverein system

Most Austrian huts are run by the Österreichischer Alpenverein (ÖAV) or German
Alpine Club (DAV). Membership pays for itself in two or three nights through
discounted beds, and it includes mountain rescue insurance across the Alps —
the single most worthwhile card a regular hut-goer can carry. Huts are graded by
category; Category I huts are remote and basic, Category II huts often have
road or lift access and more comfort.

## What a night looks like

You arrive in the afternoon, claim a bunk in a *Lager* (shared dormitory) or a
smaller *Zimmer*, hang wet gear in the drying room, and order from a menu that
will, somewhere on it, offer Kaiserschmarrn. Dinner is sociable and early.
Bring a sleeping bag liner, earplugs, and small cash notes.

## Weather and timing

The Austrian season runs roughly mid-June to late September, with the highest
huts opening last as snow clears. Afternoon thunderstorms are the defining
summer hazard — start early, aim to be off exposed ridges by early afternoon,
and treat the hut warden's forecast as the authoritative one.
