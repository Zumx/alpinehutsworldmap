The Swiss Alpine Club (SAC/CAS) operates over 150 huts across the Swiss Alps,
ranging from low valley huts accessible on a short walk to glacier outposts
at nearly 4000 m. The standard of construction and maintenance is consistently
high — solar panels, composting toilets, and rainwater collection are now
standard in newly built or renovated huts. The ten below represent the most
significant overnight positions in the country, and all are plotted on the
[map](/map).

## 1. Hörnlihütte, 3260 m — Matterhorn, Wallis

The base hut for the Hörnli Ridge — the normal route on the Matterhorn
(4478 m) — sits at 3260 m above Zermatt, reached either on foot from Schwarzsee
or by cable car to 2583 m and then a 1.5-hour approach. Capacity around 170.
The hut belongs to the Zermatt section of the SAC and is one of the most
famous mountain huts in the world. The Hörnli Ridge above is a serious
alpine route requiring a guide for most parties — the difficulty is Grade AD
with sustained exposed rock and frequent crowding. Book months in advance;
July and August dates go in spring.

## 2. Monte Rosa Hut, 2883 m — Monte Rosa massif, Wallis

The architecturally celebrated Monte Rosa Hut — a solar-powered, self-sufficient
structure completed in 2009 — sits at 2883 m on the Gorner Glacier above Zermatt,
reached in 5–6 hours from Rotenboden via the Gorner Glacier (crampons and rope
required). Capacity around 120. The hut serves as base for the Monte Rosa
massif including Dufourspitze (4634 m — the highest point in Switzerland) and
Nordend. The SAC Monte Rosa Hut is a design landmark as well as a serious
mountaineering base.

## 3. Cabane du Trient, 3170 m — Trient Plateau, Valais

On the Trient Plateau above Champex-Lac in the southwestern Valais, the Trient
is a key overnight on the Haute Route (Chamonix to Zermatt) and a staging
point for routes on the Grand Combin (4314 m) group. Reached from Champex in
about 4 hours via the Bois Median or the Fenêtre d'Arpette (a demanding
pass). The plateau approach is on glacier; crampons and rope are standard.
Capacity around 100. SAC/CAS-managed with full half-board.

## 4. Cabane des Vignettes, 3160 m — Arolla, Valais

The most dramatic position of any hut on the Haute Route — perched on a rock
island surrounded by the Pigne d'Arolla and Otemma glaciers at 3160 m. Reached
from Arolla in about 3.5 hours. Capacity around 100. Routes from here cover
the Pigne d'Arolla (3796 m) and the glacier crossing toward Cabane de Bertol
and Zermatt. A photograph of the Cabane des Vignettes against the sunrise is
one of the canonical images of Alpine hut travel.

## 5. Cabane Bertol, 3311 m — Bertol Ridge, Valais

Reached by a dramatic ladder and chain system from the Bertol col, the Bertol
hut is one of the few in the Alps that requires short technical climbing on
the approach regardless of the season. At 3311 m it commands the connection
between the Arolla glacier system and the Zermatt-facing Schönbiel glaciers.
Part of the standard Haute Route. Capacity around 80. The early-morning
departure for the Tête Blanche crossing toward the Schönbiel involves glacier
travel that is best done before solar warming softens the snow — departures
at 05:00 are normal.

## 6. Britanniahütte, 3030 m — Saas Fee, Valais

Above Saas Fee on the Hinter Allalin glacier, reached by cable car from Saas
Fee to the Felskinn station and then a 30-minute walk. At 3030 m with easy
cable-car access, the Britanniahütte is among the best acclimatization nights
in the Alps for parties preparing for higher objectives. Capacity around 120.
The surrounding peaks — Allalinhorn (4027 m), Strahlhorn (4190 m), and
Rimpfischhorn (4199 m) — are achievable from here in single days for fit,
roped parties. Full SAC half-board service.

## 7. Glecksteinhütte, 2317 m — Upper Grindelwald, Bernese Oberland

On the moraine above the Upper Grindelwald Glacier with a direct view of
the Wetterhorn (3692 m) east face, the Glecksteinhütte is one of the finest
positioned SAC huts in the Bernese Oberland. Reached from Grindelwald in 3–4
hours via the Gletscherschlucht gorge trail. Capacity around 90. The upper
Glecksteinhütte sits at the base of Wetterhorn routes and on the approach to
the Lauteraarhorn. The lower glacier approach has changed significantly with
glacial retreat — follow current route markers.

## 8. Cabane de l'Anenhütte, 2358 m — Lötschental, Valais

In the Lötschental — one of the last traditional high valleys of the Valais
— the Anenhütte serves the Lötschental Traverse and the approach to the
Bietschihorn (3934 m). The Lötschental has a distinct cultural character:
the Tschäggättä carnival masks, the isolated Baroque churches, and the sense
of a valley that retained its mountain culture longer than most. Capacity
around 60. Reached from Blatten in about 3 hours.

## 9. Cabane du Mountet, 2886 m — Val d'Anniviers, Valais

In the Val d'Anniviers above Zinal, the Mountet serves the approach to the
Zinalrothorn (4221 m) and the long ridge traverse to the Weisshorn (4506 m)
— one of the great routes in the Alps. Reached from Zinal in 3.5 hours.
Capacity around 120. The view from the hut terrace across the Mountet glacier
to the Obergabelhorn, Zinalrothorn, and Weisshorn constitutes one of the finest
mountain panoramas accessible on foot in the Alps.

## 10. Weissmies Hut (Hohsaashütte), 3100 m — Saas Grund, Valais

Above the Triftbach valley near Saas Almagell, serving as base camp for
the Weissmies (4017 m) and Lagginhorn (4010 m) — two of the more approachable
4000 m peaks in the Valais, and commonly done as a roped-but-accessible
introduction to high-alpine objectives. The Hohsaashütte is reached via cable
car to the Kreuzboden (2397 m) and a 1.5-hour walk. Capacity around 90.

## Booking Swiss SAC huts

All SAC huts are bookable through the sac-cas.ch reservation system. SAC
membership is CHF 105–170 annually depending on category and entitles the
holder to discounted overnight rates at all affiliated huts, plus reciprocal
discounts across UIAA member club huts throughout the Alps.

## The Zermatt hut cluster

The huts above Zermatt constitute the finest concentration of high-altitude
SAC huts in a single valley. Within a 15-kilometre radius of Zermatt's
main cable car, a walker with adequate mountain experience can sleep at the
Hörnlihütte (3260 m), the Monte Rosa Hut (2883 m), the Cabane des Vignettes
(3160 m), the Cabane de Bertol (3311 m), and the Schönbielhütte (2694 m).
Each serves a different objective — Matterhorn, Monte Rosa, Pigne d'Arolla,
Haute Route, Zmutt ridge — and the cluster makes multi-day routes in every
direction possible without returning to the valley.

The Cabane du Mountet (2886 m) in the Val d'Anniviers, 25 km west of Zermatt,
extends this network toward the Weisshorn (4506 m). A circuit combining
the Mountet, the Vignettes, and the Bertol — moving through the full
Pennine Alps hut chain — is one of the more ambitious multi-day objectives
in Swiss hut travel and requires glacier competence throughout.

## Bernese Oberland alternatives

The Bernese Oberland huts — Glecksteinhütte, the Lauteraarhorn area huts,
and the Lötschental huts including the Anenhütte — offer a quieter alternative
to the Zermatt circuit. The Bernese Oberland peaks (Eiger, Mönch, Jungfrau,
Finsteraarhorn, Aletschhorn) are at least as impressive as the Pennine Alps
4000ers, but the hut network is thinner and some approaches are more demanding.
The reward is genuine wilderness without the Zermatt tourist infrastructure.

The Finsteraarhornhütte (3048 m) above the Fieschergletscher is the benchmark
high hut in the Bernese Oberland — a long approach, a demanding glacier
crossing, and a summit within reach that few parties achieve. This is Swiss
high-alpine hut travel at its most serious and most rewarding.
