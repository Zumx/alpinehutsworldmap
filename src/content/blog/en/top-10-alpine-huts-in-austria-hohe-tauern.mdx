The Hohe Tauern is the great glaciated heart of the eastern Alps, a national park of over 1,800 square kilometres straddling the provinces of Salzburg, Carinthia, and the Tyrol. At its centre stands the Grossglockner, at 3,798 metres the highest mountain in Austria, its sharp twin summit rising above the Pasterze, the longest glacier in the eastern Alps. Around it cluster a dozen peaks over 3,000 metres, the highest waterfall in Austria at Krimml, and some of the densest populations of ibex, marmot, and golden eagle in the range. The huts here, built and run mostly by the Austrian and German alpine clubs, are serious high bases, many requiring glacier travel to reach the summits above them. The ten below trace the park from the Glockner to its quieter western valleys.

## 1. Erzherzog-Johann-Hütte, Grossglockner

The Erzherzog-Johann-Hütte at 3,454 metres on the Adlersruhe is the highest hut in Austria and the classic base for the Grossglockner. Perched on a rocky shoulder directly beneath the summit, it serves the rope teams that crowd the normal route to the top each summer morning. The position is extraordinary — surrounded by ice, with the Pasterze glacier sweeping away below — and the ascent to the hut already requires glacier travel and a good head for exposure. It sleeps around 95 and is one of the most atmospheric high huts in the Alps.

## 2. Stüdlhütte, Grossglockner

The Stüdlhütte at around 2,802 metres on the Fanatscharte above Kals is the modern, comfortable lower base for the Grossglockner normal route, used by most parties as the first night before moving up to the Adlersruhe. Rebuilt as an energy-efficient mountain refuge, it sleeps over 100 and fills nightly in summer. From Kals am Großglockner the approach is a steady walk through high pasture, and the hut's terrace gives a superb view of the Glockner's pyramid rising ahead — the goal for the following dawn.

## 3. Glorerhütte, Glockner Group

The Glorerhütte at around 2,642 metres sits on a saddle between the Kalser and Heiligenblut valleys, one of the oldest huts in the Glockner group and a key stage on the long-distance routes that circle the massif. It is less a summit base than a walkers' hut, ideally placed on the Wiener Höhenweg and the panoramic trails that thread the Glockner's flanks without crossing ice. The views toward the Glockner and the Schober group are exceptional, and the hut keeps a warm, traditional character.

## 4. Oberwalderhütte, Pasterze

The Oberwalderhütte at around 2,973 metres sits on the edge of the Pasterze glacier in the heart of the Glockner group, a base for glacier tours and for the Johannisberg at 3,453 metres. It is reached from the Franz-Josefs-Höhe, the famous viewpoint at the end of the Grossglockner High Alpine Road, by a glaciated approach that gives an immediate taste of the high ice. The retreat of the Pasterze is starkly visible here — the glacier has lost more than a kilometre of length in a century — making the hut a vantage point on alpine climate change.

## 5. Sudetendeutsche Hütte, Granatspitz Group

The Sudetendeutsche Hütte at around 2,650 metres lies in the Granatspitz group west of the Glockner, a quieter corner of the park between the Kalser and Stubach valleys. It serves the ascent of the Granatspitze and the long traverses that link the Glockner and Venediger groups. This is excellent walking and ski-touring country, less crowded than the Glockner itself, with high pastures rich in marmots and the chance of glimpsing the golden eagles that nest in the park. The hut is a comfortable mid-altitude base run by the Austrian Alpine Club.

## 6. Kürsinger Hütte, Grossvenediger

The Kürsinger Hütte at around 2,558 metres is the principal base for the Grossvenediger, at 3,657 metres the second-highest peak of the Tauern and one of the most beautiful glaciated summits in Austria. Set above the Obersulzbachtal, it serves the long glacier ascent of the Venediger, a classic high tour. The hut sleeps around 130 and is reached by a substantial walk up the valley. The Venediger's broad ice dome and the surrounding sea of peaks make this one of the great mountaineering corners of the eastern Alps.

## 7. Warnsdorfer Hütte, Venediger Group

The Warnsdorfer Hütte at around 2,336 metres sits in the Krimmler Achental beneath the western Venediger glaciers, near the Krimml Falls, at 380 metres the highest waterfall in Austria and one of the most visited natural sights in the country. The hut serves glacier tours on the western Venediger and the long crossing toward the Italian South Tyrol. The Krimmler Achental is a deep, forested glacial valley, and the contrast between the thundering falls at its mouth and the silent ice at its head is one of the most striking in the park.

## 8. Essener-Rostocker Hütte, Venediger Group

The Essener-Rostocker Hütte at around 2,208 metres lies in the Maurertal in the western Venediger group, a base for glacier tours and for the high crossings into the South Tyrolean Ahrntal. It sits in a deep valley surrounded by ice-hung peaks, and the approach from Hinterbichl in the Virgental is a long, beautiful walk. This is one of the wildest corners of the park, far from the road access of the Glockner, and the hut keeps the quiet, serious character of a true high base.

## 9. Badener Hütte, Venediger Group

The Badener Hütte at around 2,608 metres sits in the Frosnitztal in the eastern Venediger group, a base for the Großer Geiger and the surrounding glaciated peaks. Reached by a long walk up a remote valley, it is among the quieter high huts in the park, favoured by mountaineers who want serious glacier terrain without the crowds of the Glockner. The position deep among the ice, with marmots whistling on the moraine and the glaciers spread above, captures the essential character of the high Tauern.

## 10. Rudolfshütte, Granatspitz Group

The Rudolfshütte at around 2,315 metres above the Weißsee in the Stubach valley is one of the larger and more comfortable huts in the park, a modern alpine centre rather than a simple refuge. Reached by cable car from the valley, it serves a network of high routes in the Granatspitz group and is a popular base for both summer mountaineering and the spring ski-touring season. The Weißsee beside it and the surrounding glaciated peaks make it an accessible introduction to the high Tauern for those not yet ready for the remoter bases.
