North Macedonia's mountain landscape is defined by three major ranges and several smaller massifs arranged around the broad Vardar valley. Šar Planina (Šara) forms the northwest border with Kosovo and North Macedonia's own territory, extending from the Tetovo plain to Gostivar with summits reaching 2,748 m at Titov Vrv. In the southwest, Pelister (2,601 m) overlooks Bitola and is the centrepiece of North Macedonia's oldest national park, established in 1948. Mavrovo in the west, with its large reservoir and ski resort, rounds out the main mountain tourism areas. The планинарски дом (planinarski dom) and хижа (hizha) network in North Macedonia was developed primarily during the Yugoslav period from the 1950s through the 1980s, with many huts still operating in their original form or having undergone renovations since independence in 1991. The country also benefits from growing international trekking interest due to the Balkan Mountain Trail network passing through several of these ranges.

## 1. Планинарски Дом Даре Џамбаз, Šar Planina

Планинарски дом Даре Џамбаз is the principal mountain house on Šar Planina, located near Popova Šapka ski resort at around 1,780 m. Named after a Macedonian mountaineer, the dom was one of the most active huts in Yugoslavia and continues to serve as a base for summit bids on the Šar Planina peaks. Titov Vrv, the highest point at 2,748 m, is accessible from this area in a long day. The surrounding high plateau holds numerous glacial lakes, including Bogovinsko jezero and Crno jezero.

## 2. Хотел Изгрев, Pelister

Хотел Изгрев is positioned on Pelister mountain above Bitola, in the heart of the Pelister National Park. At around 1,600 m, it stands in the subalpine zone below the high peaks and overlooks the Bitola plain to the south. Pelister is famous for its Molika pine (Pinus peuce), a Balkan endemic species with five-needle clusters that covers the slopes between 1,200 and 2,000 m. The summit of Pelister at 2,601 m is accessible from the hotel in around four hours and holds two small glacial lakes — Malo and Golemo Ezero — known as the "Pelister Eyes."

## 3. Копанки, Šar Planina

Хижа Копанки sits at around 1,500 m on the southeastern slopes of Šar Planina above the village of Brodec near Tetovo. This hut is one of the lower approaches to the Šar Planina massif from the Macedonian side and serves day hikers from Tetovo as well as parties beginning the multi-day Šar traverse. The trail upward from Kopanki enters open beech forest and transitions to high pasture above 1,700 m.

## 4. Длабочица, Mavrovo

Длабочица is a mountain shelter in the Mavrovo National Park area, established in 1949 and covering roughly 730 square kilometres — the largest national park in North Macedonia. The park contains the Mavrovo reservoir, the Radika river canyon, and the village of Janče, known for its medieval church. The shelter at Dlabochitsa serves hikers on the Bistra massif (2,163 m), the main summit in the Mavrovo area, and is positioned in the mixed forest zone below the treeline.

## 5. Шарски Води, Šar Planina

Шарски Води — "Šar Waters" — is a hut near the major spring systems on the western section of Šar Planina above Gostivar. The area is notable for the quality and abundance of its water sources, which give the hut its name. The surrounding high pastures were traditionally used for transhumant sheep grazing from villages in the Gostivar valley, and the old stone shepherd shelters are still visible on the plateau. The hut is a base for the western Šar peaks including Piribeg (2,524 m).

## 6. Вртешка, Central North Macedonia

Хижа Вртешка is a mountain shelter in the central ranges of North Macedonia near the Suva Gora massif west of Skopje. Suva Gora is the closest mountain area to the capital for day hikers and weekend visitors, rising to around 1,700 m above the Vardar valley. The hut has basic facilities and is maintained by one of the Skopje-based mountaineering clubs. The trails around Vrteshka connect to a broader ridge network providing full-day circular routes.

## 7. Калабастер, Šar Planina

Калабастер is positioned on the high Šar plateau above Tetovo at around 2,100 m, among the glacial lakes and rocky cirques of the upper massif. At this altitude the landscape is alpine and exposed, with summer wildflower meadows dominated by gentians and mountain avens. The hut provides basic shelter and is primarily a waypoint on the Šar ridge traverse rather than a full overnight accommodation facility. The surrounding lakes are popular fishing destinations for the alpine char that inhabit several of the Šar tarns.

## 8. Горна Белица, Mavrovo

Горна Белица ("Upper Belitsa") is a mountain shelter and cultural heritage site near the village of Gorна Belica in the Mavrovo area, known for its well-preserved Ottoman-era architecture and traditional weaving culture. The shelter serves hikers approaching the Stogovo massif (2,273 m) from the west. Stogovo is a broad, forested ridge with extensive high pastures that was traditionally one of the most important summer grazing areas in western North Macedonia.

## 9. Љуботен, Šar Planina

Хижа Љуботен is the base hut for the ascent of Ljuboten (2,498 m), the most prominent peak of Šar Planina visible from Skopje. Ljuboten sits at the southeastern tip of the range, separated from the main massif by a deep saddle, and commands extraordinary views across the Skopje basin. The hut at 1,700 m on the approach trail from the Gjorce Petrov municipality is the standard overnight stop before early morning summit ascents. The trail is one of the most popular in North Macedonia.

## 10. Матка, Skopje Canyon

Матка is not a highland hut in the traditional sense but rather the canyon resort and shelter complex in the Matka gorge, 15 km southwest of Skopje. The Treska river has carved a spectacular limestone gorge here, and the area contains five cave systems, a hydroelectric reservoir, a medieval monastery, and a network of via ferrata routes. The mountain shelter and hostel at Matka serves climbers and canyon hikers and is the most visited outdoor destination in North Macedonia due to its proximity to the capital.

## Plan Your North Macedonia Mountain Trips on the Map

North Macedonia's mountain hut network spans three major ranges spread across the western third of the country, with each range offering a distinct character — the high alpine environment of Šar Planina, the Molika-pine forests of Pelister, and the canyon and reservoir scenery of Mavrovo.
