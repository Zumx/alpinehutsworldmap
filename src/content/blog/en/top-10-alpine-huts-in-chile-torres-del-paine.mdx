Torres del Paine National Park in Chilean Patagonia protects roughly 242,000 hectares of terrain dominated by the Paine Massif — a cluster of granite towers, horns, and glaciers that has become one of the most recognisable mountain landscapes on earth. The W Circuit, a four-to-five-day trek along the base of the massif, and the longer O Circuit adding the full back loop require nine to twelve days and are among the most heavily regulated long-distance hikes in South America. The Chilean government and park concessionaire CONAF strictly control overnight capacity inside the park; all hikers must book both campsites and refugio beds through the two main operators — Fantástico Sur and Vertice Patagonia — months in advance for the November through March peak season. Beyond Torres del Paine, Chilean Patagonia contains a scattering of mountain shelters in the Llanquihue, Puyehue, and northern Patagonian lake zones, reaching down toward the Austral Highway.

## 1. Refugio Chileno, Valle del Ascencio

Refugio Chileno sits at approximately 1,070 metres in the Valle del Ascencio, the approach valley for the famous viewpoint of the Torres towers themselves. It is operated by Fantástico Sur and sleeps around 60 in a modern heated building with a full meal service. The hut is the mandatory overnight stop for walkers making the pre-dawn start to reach the Mirador Las Torres by sunrise — a four-hour round trip on a steep moraine trail. The surroundings include an ice-melt stream and southern beech forest that begins thinning out above the hut. Booking opens in mid-July for the following season and the earliest dates sell within hours.

## 2. Refugio Agostini, Lago Grey

Named after the Italian Salesian priest and explorer Alberto María de Agostini, who produced some of the earliest detailed maps of Patagonian geography in the 1940s, Refugio Agostini is positioned near the shore of Lago Grey with frontal views of the Grey Glacier's calving face. The glacial lake carries floating icebergs year-round, their blue tones most vivid in the low afternoon sun. The hut provides 40 beds, full meals, and a terrace from which walkers watch the ice. It sits on the W Circuit's western arm and marks the turnaround point for most walkers before they loop back toward the Central Zone.

## 3. Refugio Toni Rohrer, Región de Los Lagos

Outside the Torres del Paine park boundary, Refugio Toni Rohrer operates near Cochamo in the Región de Los Lagos, roughly 300 kilometres north of the park. The valley is accessed from the small town of Cochamo by a muddy six-to-eight-hour trail through dense temperate rainforest. The refugio sits at the edge of La Junta meadow at around 600 metres and serves as a base for rock climbers working the enormous granite walls that have been compared to Yosemite in scale. Named after a German settler who developed the trail in the early twentieth century, the hut is privately run and sleeps about 30. Dates from October to April are the most practical.

## 4. Refugio de Montaña Cerro Provincia, Santiago hinterland

Cerro Provincia rises to 2,751 metres northeast of Santiago in the Yerba Loca protected area, and Refugio de Montaña Cerro Provincia serves the mountaineers and ski tourers who use this relatively accessible Andean peak as a training ground. The hut sits at around 2,200 metres and is operated by a mountaineering club with limited beds — typically 15 to 20. Access from the trailhead at the end of Yerba Loca valley takes five to six hours. The winter and spring ski-touring season, from July to October, generates most overnight demand; summer use is lighter.

## 5. Refugio Volcán Calbuco, Chilean Lake District

Volcán Calbuco erupted dramatically in April 2015 after 43 years of dormancy, reshuffling the access and trail infrastructure around its flanks. Refugio Volcán Calbuco, rebuilt and reopened after the eruption, sits at around 1,600 metres on the northwest approach and offers 20 beds for the summit approach. The volcano stands 2,003 metres and requires a predawn start from the refugio to reach the crater rim before afternoon cloud closes in. The Lake District setting, with Lago Llanquihue visible to the north on clear days, is exceptional. Permission from ONEMI and SERNAGEOMIN is required before ascent.

## 6. Refugio El Caulle, Puyehue-Cordón Caulle

The Cordón Caulle volcano complex near Lago Ranco also erupted in 2011, and Refugio El Caulle at around 1,400 metres remains one of the few operational shelters in this geologically active zone. The approach crosses lava fields from the 2011 eruption — a desolate landscape of black basalt overlaying what were once beech forests. The refugio is basic, sleeping 15 to 20 in utilitarian conditions, and serves walkers crossing the area on route to Argentina's Neuquén province. Water sources in the lava field are limited and must be carried from the shelter.

## 7. Refugio Charles, Parque Pumalín

Parque Pumalín, created by Doug Tompkins and now incorporated into the Chilean national park system as Parque Pumalín Douglas Tompkins, contains several refugios along its trail network south of Chaitén. Refugio Charles is a well-built timber shelter in the park at around 800 metres, serving walkers on the Sendero de Chile section that runs through the park. The building is named for an early settler of the Pumalín area and sleeps 20 with a functional kitchen. The surrounding temperate rainforest, with alerce trees (Fitzroya cupressoides) estimated at 3,000 years old, makes the environment extraordinary.

## 8. Ranger Hut, Torres del Paine O Circuit

The back section of the O Circuit in Torres del Paine passes through several CONAF ranger stations that include basic sleeping areas — typically corrugated iron buildings with wood stoves and 8 to 12 beds — for use by rangers and permitted walkers. These ranger huts at Paso John Gardner and Perros campsite area fill gaps between the staffed Vertice and Fantástico Sur refugios on the back loop. They charge a nominal fee and provide minimal services; walkers must carry their own food. The environment on the back loop is more exposed and less visited than the W, with views directly onto the Southern Patagonian Ice Field.

## 9. Refugio Puesto Rivera, Tierra del Fuego

Tierra del Fuego's mountain shelters are sparse, but Refugio Puesto Rivera provides overnight accommodation for walkers exploring the Fuegian Andes near Ushuaia. The building is a former cattle outstation converted to trekker use, sitting at around 500 metres in a broad glacial valley. The approach involves river crossings through southern beech forest. Because of the extreme weather volatility — storms can arrive within hours at any time of year — the hut provides an essential safety margin for the Dientes de Navarino circuit, the southernmost trekking route in the world.

## 10. Refugio Plantat, Laguna San Rafael area

Laguna San Rafael National Park, inscribed as a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve, centres on the San Rafael Glacier where calving icebergs enter the lagoon. Refugio Plantat serves researchers and adventure travelers in this remote park, accessible only by boat or light aircraft from Puerto Montt or Coyhaique. The hut sits at sea level near the lagoon shore. This is not an alpine hut in the traditional elevation sense, but the glaciological and wilderness environment place it firmly in the category of mountain shelter rather than coastal lodge. Visitor numbers are strictly limited, and stays must be coordinated through CONAF's regional office.
