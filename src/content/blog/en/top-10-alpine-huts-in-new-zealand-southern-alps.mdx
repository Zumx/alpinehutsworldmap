New Zealand's Great Walks are a network of nine premier tramping routes managed by the Department of Conservation (DOC), spread across both the North and South Islands. Six of the nine Great Walks traverse the mountains, fiords, and beech forests of the South Island's Fiordland and Mount Aspiring national parks, which together protect the western flank of the Southern Alps. The hut system on the Great Walks is among the best-maintained in the world: purpose-built facilities with flush toilets, gas cooking, heating, and resident wardens operate on each route. Great Walk hut passes are required and must be booked in advance; peak season runs from late October through April. Out of season, the huts revert to basic "off-season" status with reduced facilities and cheaper Backcountry Hut tickets. The booking system — which opened the DOC website to enormous traffic when it launched for the 2003 season — has since moved fully online, and popular dates on the Milford Track sell out within minutes of the booking window opening in early June each year.

## 1. Clinton Hut, Milford Track

Clinton Hut at 145 metres above sea level on the Milford Track is the first overnight hut on what is often described as the finest walk in the world. It sits in the narrow glacial trough of the Clinton valley, three to four kilometres beyond the ferry landing at Glade Wharf. The hut sleeps 40 in bunk rooms and has been rebuilt to the high DOC Great Walk standard. The Clinton River flows directly beside the hut, filled with extraordinarily clear water from the Milford rainforest. The valley walls rise nearly 1,500 metres on both sides, channelling wind and rain in the unpredictable Fiordland manner.

## 2. Glade House, Milford Track start

Glade House, at the head of Lake Te Anau near the ferry landing, is a private lodge rather than a DOC hut, but it serves as the acclimatisation point for guided walkers beginning the Milford Track. For independent walkers, Clinton Hut is the first official stop, but Glade House represents the spirit of the track's beginning: the transition from vehicle access to the wilderness of the Clinton valley. The lodge has been operated by Fiordland-based guiding companies for over a century.

## 3. Pompolona Lodge, Milford Track

Pompolona Lodge on the Milford Track is a private guided-walk lodge at approximately 600 metres in the upper Clinton valley, open only to guests on the Milford Track guided walk operated by Ultimate Hikes. Independent walkers overnight at the nearby DOC Mintaro Hut instead. Pompolona was constructed with considerable investment in the 1990s and provides en-suite rooms, a bar, and multi-course dining — a striking contrast to the DOC hut experience. The building's name derives from an early pastoral lease in the valley.

## 4. Routeburn Falls Hut, Routeburn Track

Routeburn Falls Hut at 1,005 metres is the highest hut on the Routeburn Track and one of the most scenically positioned of any DOC facility. It sits directly beside the Routeburn Falls, where the Routeburn River drops over open rock slabs in a series of cascades visible from the hut windows. The two-storey hut sleeps 48 in Great Walk standard accommodation. The Routeburn Track connects the Dart-Rees valley with the Hollyford valley across a pass at 1,277 metres, and the Falls Hut is on the eastern approach side. Rain — the Routeburn averages over 9 metres annually — can arrive within minutes at any time of year.

## 5. Routeburn Flats Hut, Routeburn Track

Routeburn Flats Hut at 590 metres sits in the broad valley floor where the Routeburn River meanders through a rare open flat among Fiordland's typically steep terrain. The hut sleeps 48 and marks the lower approach for walkers starting the Routeburn from the Queenstown end. The red beech (Nothofagus fusca) forest surrounding the flats is particularly impressive in evening light. The Flats were historically grazed under pastoral lease, and the recovery of native vegetation since the grazing ended in the 1970s is visible in the diversity of the understorey.

## 6. Luxmore Hut, Kepler Track

Luxmore Hut at 1,085 metres on the Kepler Track above Lake Te Anau is the largest hut on any New Zealand Great Walk, sleeping 54 guests. The position on the Luxmore Bench — a tilted limestone terrace with views over Lake Te Anau and the Murchison Mountains — is exceptional. The Kepler Track opened in 1988 as New Zealand's first purpose-built Great Walk and was designed specifically to relieve pressure on the Milford Track. The ridge traverse above Luxmore, connecting to Mount Luxmore (1,472 m), provides the most exposed and spectacular section of any South Island Great Walk.

## 7. Iris Burn Hut, Kepler Track

Iris Burn Hut at 590 metres in the Iris Burn valley is the western overnight stop on the Kepler Track circuit. The hut sleeps 54 and sits beside the Iris Burn stream in a narrow forested gorge. The descent from the exposed Kepler ridge to this valley is one of the more dramatic elevation changes on any Great Walk — from 1,400 metres on the ridge to 590 metres at the hut in around four hours. Evening kiwi calls are often heard near the hut; the adjacent Murchison Mountains are home to the last wild takahe population, managed by DOC since the species' rediscovery in 1948.

## 8. Mintaro Hut, Milford Track

Mintaro Hut at 560 metres in the upper Clinton valley is the penultimate DOC hut before the Milford Track's highest point — Mackinnon Pass at 1,154 metres. It sleeps 40 and serves as the crucial overnight before the long Mackinnon Pass day, which at 15 to 18 kilometres is the most physically demanding on the track. The Clinton valley at this point is hemmed by waterfalls descending from the surrounding peaks; Sutherland Falls — at 580 metres one of the tallest waterfalls in New Zealand — is a side trip from the next hut, Dumpling Hut.

## 9. Boyle Base Hut, Lewis Pass

Boyle Base Hut at around 550 metres near Lewis Pass in the northern Southern Alps is a DOC backcountry hut serving the St. James Walkway — one of New Zealand's former Great Walks that was removed from that category and redesignated as a backcountry route. The hut sleeps 24 and provides basic facilities characteristic of standard DOC backcountry huts: gas cookers, sleeping platforms with mattresses, and cold running water. Lewis Pass is one of the lowest mountain passes through the Southern Alps at 864 metres, giving the area a milder character than the Fiordland routes to the south.

## 10. H.E.L. Porter Memorial Lodge, Arthur's Pass

The H.E.L. Porter Memorial Lodge at approximately 1,100 metres near Arthur's Pass is operated by the Canterbury Mountaineering Club (CMC) and named after Harry Ernest Lear Porter, an early New Zealand climber who died on the Bavarian Alps in 1929. It sits in the steep valleys north of the Arthur's Pass township and provides accommodation for climbers and ski tourers working the Craigieburn Range. The lodge sleeps 20 and is used year-round; winter ski-touring demand on the Craigieburn slopes drives the December-August occupancy.

## Explore the Full Map

New Zealand's Great Walk hut system represents a specific model of mountain accommodation — purpose-built, intensively managed, and requiring advance booking — that differs fundamentally from the European alpine hut tradition or the Japanese yamagoya system. All DOC Great Walk huts, backcountry huts, and private lodges on or near the South Island's mountain walking routes are plotted with booking links, seasonal operating status, and access information at [Open the map](/map). The Great Walks booking window — which opens in early June each year for the following summer season — marks the beginning of the New Zealand mountain walking calendar, and consulting [the map](/map) in advance helps identify which routes require the earliest booking and which alternatives exist when the primary huts are full.
