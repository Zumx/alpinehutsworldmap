The Bernese Oberland is home to some of the most recognisable mountain architecture on earth — the north faces of the Eiger, Mönch, and Jungfrau, the vast Aletsch Glacier flowing south from the Jungfraujoch, and the deeply cut valleys of Grindelwald and Lauterbrunnen that have drawn visitors for two centuries. The SAC (Swiss Alpine Club) huts in this region are typically well-staffed, carefully maintained, and positioned at altitude to provide access to glaciated terrain that demands ice axe and crampon competence from June through September. These ten represent some of the finest high bases in the western Swiss Alps.

## 1. Glecksteinhütte, Grindelwald

At 2,317 metres on a rocky spur above the Upper Grindelwald Glacier, the Glecksteinhütte offers one of the most intimate glacier views of any SAC hut in the Bernese Oberland. The approach from Grindelwald takes around four hours, climbing steeply through forest and then over moraine to the hut's exposed position. The Gleckstein is used primarily as a base for routes on the Wetterhorn (3,692 metres) and the Rosenhorn (3,689 metres), both moderate mountaineering objectives for parties with good snow and ice skills. The hut sleeps around 50 guests and opens from mid-July to mid-September.

## 2. Schreckhornhütte, Grindelwald

The Schreckhornhütte sits at 2,529 metres in the high terrain north of the Schreckhorn (4,078 metres), one of the most technically demanding 4,000-metre peaks in Switzerland. The approach from the Gleckstein glacier system or from the Strahlegg pass route is serious, requiring glacier travel and crevasse awareness. The hut sleeps approximately 30 people and is managed by the SAC section Grindelwald. It is not a casual destination — most parties arriving here are committed to an attempt on the Schreckhorn or a traverse of the Lauteraarhorn.

## 3. Lauteraarjochhütte, Grimsel region

Positioned at 2,392 metres on the Lauteraar glacier between the Schreckhorn and the Finsteraarhorn (4,274 metres, the highest summit in the Bernese Alps), the Lauteraarjochhütte is among the most remote SAC huts in the region. Access requires crossing the lower Lauteraar glacier from the Grimsel pass road, and the approach takes around three to four hours. The hut was rebuilt in the early 2000s and sleeps around 50. It serves as a key waypoint on traverses of the central Bernese high route and for serious mountaineers approaching the Finsteraarhorn from the north.

## 4. Blüemlisalphütte, Kandersteg

The Blüemlisalphütte at 2,840 metres above Kandersteg is one of the highest SAC huts in the Bernese Oberland and the central base for the Blüemlisalp massif, a compact group of glaciated peaks reaching 3,664 metres. The approach from Kandersteg via the Oeschinensee takes about four to five hours and is a classic route: first through forest and meadow above the famous turquoise lake, then over steep moraine to the hut. The glacier routes from here onto the Blüemlisalphorn and Morgenhorn are well-suited to parties with modest mountaineering experience.

## 5. Balmhornhütte, Kandersteg

At approximately 2,457 metres above the Gastern valley near Kandersteg, the Balmhornhütte provides access to the Balmhorn (3,698 metres) and the Altels (3,629 metres), two substantial glaciated summits on the southern rim of the Bernese Alps. The approach from Kandersteg is around four hours. The hut is relatively small — sleeping around 40 — and tends to attract more experienced mountaineers than the larger, more accessible huts on the Eiger side. The Gastern valley itself is uncommonly beautiful, a steep-sided trough carved by glacial ice.

## 6. Almagelleralp, Saas Valley

Technically belonging to the Valais side of the Alps, the Almagelleralp at around 2,195 metres above Saas-Almagell serves as a gateway to the Portjengrat ridge and the terrain bordering the Bernese watershed. The alp is more accessible than the high glacier huts and provides a comfortable mid-level base for hikers exploring the Saas Valley circuit, which includes views toward the Weissmies (4,017 metres) to the east.

## 7. Doldenhornjochhütte, Kandersteg area

Sitting at around 2,643 metres on the Doldenhornjoch between the Fisistock and the broader Kandersteg hut network, this smaller SAC shelter is used primarily by parties crossing between the Gastern valley and the Lötschental to the south. The crossing is a full-day undertaking with glacier terrain and requires appropriate equipment. The hut is a basic facility, staffed intermittently in summer, and sees far less traffic than the Blüemlisalp or Kandersteg mainline routes.

## 8. Grimselhospiz, Grimsel Pass

The Grimselhospiz at 1,908 metres is not a mountaineering base in the traditional sense, but its extraordinary position on a rocky promontory above the Grimselsee reservoir makes it one of the most atmospheric overnight stops in the Swiss Alps. The original hospice was flooded when the reservoir was built in the 1930s; the current building, perched on a rock spur above the water, has operated as a hotel and mountain inn since then. The Grimsel pass road reaches 2,164 metres and is typically open from May to October.

## 9. Rottalehütte, Jungfrau region

The Rottalehütte at 3,297 metres is one of the highest huts in the Bernese Oberland, positioned on the southwestern flanks of the Jungfrau (4,158 metres) above the Rottal glacier. Access from Stechelberg in the Lauterbrunnen valley involves a long approach of around six to seven hours. The hut is used as a base for routes on the Jungfrau from the south — a less-crowded alternative to approaches via the Jungfraujoch railway. It sleeps around 40 and is staffed in July and August.

## 10. Wildstrubelehütte, Wildstrubel massif

The Wildstrubelehütte at around 2,793 metres sits on the eastern shoulder of the Wildstrubel (3,243 metres), the dominant summit of the range between Kandersteg and the Lenk in the Simmental. The hut is a SAC facility sleeping roughly 55 and serves as the main base for ski touring on the Wildstrubel in spring — the area is part of the Haute Route Ski de Randonnée circuit. In summer, routes from the hut connect to the Plaine Morte glacier and the Crans-Montana area to the south.
