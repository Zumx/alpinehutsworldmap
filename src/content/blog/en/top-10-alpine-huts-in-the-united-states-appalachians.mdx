The Appalachian Mountain Club (AMC) hut system in New Hampshire's White
Mountains is the oldest and most developed mountain hut network in the United
States, with eight staffed huts in the Presidential Range and surrounding
peaks that are fully operational from June to September and open in caretaker
or self-service mode in shoulder seasons. The system is designed around one-
day walks between huts; the White Mountain network offers the closest equivalent
to Alpine hut-to-hut travel available in the eastern United States.

## 1. Lakes of the Clouds Hut, 1534 m — Mount Washington, New Hampshire

The largest and most famous AMC hut, Lakes of the Clouds at 1534 m sits on
the Crawford Path below the summit of Mount Washington (1917 m — the highest
peak in the northeastern United States, infamous for some of the most severe
weather in the world). Capacity around 90. The hut's position on the exposed
Presidential Range ridge means it is routinely subject to 100 km/h winds, fog,
and rapidly changing conditions. Open June to mid-September; caretaker service
September–mid-October. Summit access via the Crawford Path takes under 2 hours
from the hut; a summit day from the hut is the standard AMC Presidential
experience.

## 2. Madison Spring Hut, 1436 m — Madison-Adams col, New Hampshire

In the col between Mount Madison (1636 m) and Mount Adams (1446 m) — the second
and fourth highest peaks in the Northeast — the Madison Spring Hut at 1436 m
is perhaps the most dramatically exposed of the AMC huts. The wind exposure
in this col is exceptional; the hut is built low and solid. Capacity around 50.
Reached from the Appalachian Trail via the Valley Way (3–4 hours from Appalachia
parking) or from the Pinkham Notch direction via Air Line/Gulfside. Open June
to mid-September.

## 3. Mizpah Spring Hut, 1219 m — Mounts Clinton and Jackson col, New Hampshire

On the southern Presidential Range traverse, Mizpah Spring Hut at 1219 m
serves the Crawford Notch approach and the ridge walk toward Lakes of the
Clouds. At a lower elevation than the northern huts and in a more sheltered
position, it has a less severe character but provides important overnight
capacity on the southern Presidential traverse. Capacity around 60.

## 4. Carter Notch Hut, 1006 m — Carter Range, New Hampshire

The lowest-elevation AMC hut, Carter Notch sits at 1006 m in the col between
Wildcat Mountain and Carter Dome, below two mountain ponds in a narrow notch.
One of the most remote huts in the system — the approach via the 19 Mile Brook
Trail from Route 16 takes 3 hours. Open year-round in self-service mode (winter)
and staffed in summer season. The Carter Range traverse from Wildcat to North
Carter and the Nineteen Mile Brook circuit are the primary routes.

## 5. Greenleaf Hut, 1316 m — Franconia Ridge, New Hampshire

Below the Franconia Ridge — one of the most-walked ridgelines in New England,
including the famous Franconia Ridge Loop via Lincoln and Lafayette summits —
the Greenleaf Hut at 1316 m serves the approach from Franconia Notch State
Park. Capacity around 46. Reached from the Lafayette Place campground in
approximately 2.5 hours. The Greenleaf-to-Galehead traverse over the Garfield
Ridge is a challenging full-day section of the Appalachian Trail.

## 6. Galehead Hut, 1310 m — Twin Range, New Hampshire

On the Garfield Ridge between South Twin Mountain (1375 m) and Galehead
Mountain, the Galehead Hut at 1310 m is one of the more remote AMC huts —
the minimum approach is 4 miles from the nearest trailhead. Capacity around 36.
The Twin-Range traverse and the climb to South Twin provide excellent high-
elevation walking in a less-crowded area than the Presidentials.

## 7. Zealand Falls Hut, 762 m — Crawford Notch area, New Hampshire

The Zealand Falls Hut at the lowest elevation in the system (762 m) is reached
by a gentle 2.7-mile trail from the Zealand Trailhead — one of the most
accessible overnight introductions to the AMC hut system for families and
first-time hut users. A small waterfall and the Zealand valley ponds make it
a peaceful option. Open year-round in self-service mode.

## 8. Lonesome Lake Hut, 905 m — Franconia Notch, New Hampshire

Above Franconia Notch State Park's Echo Lake, the Lonesome Lake Hut at 905 m
is one of the most popular family-friendly AMC huts — an easy 1.6-mile trail
suitable for children, with the lake reflecting Cannon Mountain and the
Franconia Ridge. Capacity around 44. Open year-round.

## 9. Pinkham Notch Visitor Center, 605 m — AMC Base, New Hampshire

Not a backcountry hut but the AMC's main visitor centre and hostel at Pinkham
Notch, serving as the trailhead for Mount Washington's Tuckerman Ravine approach.
The Visitor Center provides hostel accommodation, food, gear retail, and
weather information — the nerve centre of AMC operations. Open year-round.

## 10. Joe Dodge Lodge, 605 m — Pinkham Notch

Adjacent to the Visitor Center, Joe Dodge Lodge provides bunkroom and private
room accommodation at the Pinkham Notch base with hot meals — the valley-level
complement to the high-ridge huts. Named after the legendary AMC hut manager
Joe Dodge, who ran the hut system from 1922 to 1959 and defined its culture.

## Mount Washington and its weather record

The Lakes of the Clouds Hut sits at 1533 m on the flanks of Mount Washington
(1917 m) — a modest elevation by Alpine standards but a peak with one of
the most extreme weather records in the world. The summit weather observatory
has recorded sustained wind speeds of 327 km/h (the world wind speed record
from 1934 until 2010) and temperatures as low as -40°C. The Appalachians
lack the altitude of the Alps but compensate with the full intensity of North
Atlantic weather systems that arrive unimpeded across the ocean.

The Presidential Range, of which Washington is the highest, typically receives
its first significant snow in October and retains snow until late May. The
summer season for the AMC huts is approximately June through mid-October.
In winter, the above-treeline Presidentials carry hurricane-force winds on
many days; the winter mountaineering season requires full cold-weather
expedition capability.

The Tuckerman Ravine on the east face of Washington — a glacial cirque that
holds snow well into spring — is a celebrated destination for spring skiing
and ice climbing. The ravine's headwall steepens to 40–55 degrees; several
spring skiers die in Tuckerman Ravine annually in avalanche accidents or
from falls on icy conditions.

## AMC hut booking

AMC hut reservations are made through the AMC reservation system (outdoors.org).
The huts are open from approximately late June to mid-October; a specific
end date varies by hut and by year. The popular July and August weekends at
Lakes of the Clouds — the AMC system's most visited hut — book out months
in advance. The shoulder season (late June and late September) offers better
availability, cooler temperatures, and smaller crowds.

AMC members receive a discount on hut rates. Annual AMC membership is
approximately USD 65 for adults and includes discounts on hut stays,
programming, and publications. The hut system's full-service model (beds
plus two meals) is distinctive among American mountain infrastructure; the
cost (approximately USD 150–200 per person per night including meals in 2025)
reflects the staffed, full-provision character that has no parallel in other
American mountain systems.
