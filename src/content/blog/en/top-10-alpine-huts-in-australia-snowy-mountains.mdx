Australia's alpine zone is modest by world standards — the highest peak,
Mount Kosciuszko, reaches only 2228 m — but it is the continent's only
subalpine environment and it is significant ecologically, historically, and
culturally. The Kosciuszko National Park in the Snowy Mountains of New South
Wales encompasses the Main Range, Australia's only true alpine terrain above
the snowline, and a network of historic huts built over two centuries of
high-country use. Some were cattlemen's huts from the era of summer grazing;
others were built more recently as emergency and recreation shelters.

## 1. Seamans Hut, 2020 m — Main Range, Kosciuszko

Seamans Hut is the most historically significant emergency shelter on the Main
Range, built in 1929 as a memorial to Max Seaman and Sydney Easte, who died
in a blizzard on the Main Range in 1928. At 2020 m, it is an emergency shelter
(not a staffed hut) capable of accommodating approximately 8 people. The hut
has been credited with saving lives in subsequent storms; it is maintained by
the Kosciuszko Huts Association. The location above the Merritts Nature Track
makes it accessible from Perisher Valley and the Charlotte Pass area.

## 2. Cootapatamba Hut, 2000 m — Main Range

Near Lake Cootapatamba — the highest lake in Australia (2038 m) below the
Main Range ridge — the Cootapatamba Hut is a stone emergency shelter maintained
by the Kosciuszko Huts Association. The lake is a glacial relict; the
landscape above 1900 m on the Main Range is genuinely alpine in character.
The approach from Charlotte Pass (1765 m) follows the Summit Walk, which also
leads to Mount Kosciuszko.

## 3. Whites River Hut, 1610 m — Main Range, western flank

A historic stone hut at 1610 m on the western flank of the Main Range, Whites
River Hut is one of the oldest surviving high-country huts in the Kosciuszko
area. Built by the Snowy Mountains Authority in the 1940s, it serves backcountry
skiers and summer bushwalkers exploring the area between Guthega and the western
slopes.

## 4. Perisher Valley Hut Network, 1720 m — Perisher Valley

The Perisher Valley area — Australia's largest ski resort — has several historic
huts in the surrounding backcountry terrain that predate the ski resort
development. The Club Lake Hut (approaching the summit route from the east)
and associated structures are maintained by the national park.

## 5. Twynam Hut area, 1950 m — Mount Twynam, Main Range

Near the second highest peak in Australia, Mount Twynam (2196 m), emergency
shelter facilities on the approach routes provide the basic infrastructure
for multi-day traverses on the Main Range.

## 6. Round Mountain Hut, 1400 m — Snowy Mountains, northwest approaches

One of the surviving cattlemen's huts in the lower-elevation terrain of the
Snowy Mountains, Round Mountain Hut at 1400 m is typical of the timber
slab construction used by high-country graziers from the mid-19th century.
The Kosciuszko Huts Association has restored many of these structures as
part of a broader effort to maintain the region's pastoral heritage.

## 7. Dead Horse Gap Shelter, 1580 m — Thredbo Valley, NSW

Near the Dead Horse Gap on the Alpine Way between Thredbo and Jindabyne, a
basic shelter provides emergency refuge at the road-accessible gap. The area
is a popular entry point for backcountry skiers and winter walkers heading
into the Main Range from the south.

## 8. Davys Hut, 1600 m — Main Range area, ACT boundary

One of the historic high-country huts near the ACT-NSW border area, Davys Hut
represents the pastoral tradition of the Monaro tablelands that extended into
the high country. Many of these huts are now protected heritage structures.

## 9. Brindabella Range Huts, 1000–1400 m — ACT/NSW border

The Brindabella Range west of Canberra has several historic huts within the
Namadgi National Park — lower elevation than the Snowy Mountains Main Range
but part of the same high-country cattlemen's tradition. The Orroral Valley
area preserves several examples.

## 10. Barry Way Emergency Shelter, 1100 m — Snowy Mountains, Victoria border

On the remote Barry Way between Khancoban and Thredbo, a basic emergency
shelter provides protection for parties caught by unexpected weather in the
transition zone between the NSW Snowy Mountains and the Victorian Alps.

## The Kosciuszko Huts Association

The Kosciuszko Huts Association (KHA) is the organisation responsible for
maintaining historic huts in the Kosciuszko National Park. Founded in 1971,
the KHA is a volunteer group that undertakes annual maintenance work — replacing
roof iron, repairing walls, restoring fireplaces — to keep the historic high-
country huts in usable condition. Many of the huts that would otherwise have
fallen into dereliction have been preserved through their work.

The KHA maintains a list of huts under their care with GPS coordinates and
access notes; the huts vary from well-maintained stone structures to simple
timber shelters requiring significant caution before use. The organisation
also provides historical research on the pastoral history of the Snowy Mountains
and the families whose lives revolved around the high-country grazing tradition.

## Weather in the Snowy Mountains

The Kosciuszko alpine area receives reliable winter snowfall — averaging 200 cm
at the summit plateaux — and can produce dangerous conditions in all seasons.
The Australian alpine zone is notorious for rapid weather deterioration; the
storm that killed Max Seaman and Sydney Easte in 1928 (the incident that
prompted the building of Seamans Hut) came in November, in what was nominally
the early summer season.

Summer conditions (December–February) are generally reliable for walking but
can include afternoon thunderstorms, hail, and rapid temperature drops.
Always carry waterproof layers, warm clothes, and navigation equipment on
any route above 1800 m regardless of the morning forecast.

Winter conditions (June–September) at higher elevations require full alpine
equipment. The ski resorts of Perisher, Thredbo, and Charlotte Pass operate
the lift infrastructure; backcountry ski touring in the Main Range is possible
but avalanche risk assessment applies to the steeper terrain.

## The Main Range traverse

The full Main Range traverse from Charlotte Pass to Dead Horse Gap (or the
reverse) is one of the finest multi-day walking routes in Australia. At
approximately 22 km, it follows the ridge above 1900 m for most of its length,
passing both Mount Kosciuszko (2228 m) and the secondary peaks of Townsend
(2209 m) and Abbott (2145 m). Emergency shelter (Seamans Hut, Cootapatamba,
and several others) is available along the route.

## The Snowy Mountains as a water catchment

The Kosciuszko National Park exists not only for wilderness recreation but
as the primary water catchment for the Snowy Mountains Hydro-Electric Scheme —
one of the largest renewable energy and irrigation infrastructure projects
in Australian history. Built between 1949 and 1974, the Snowy Scheme
diverts water from the Snowy and Eucumbene rivers westward through tunnels
under the Main Range to irrigate the Murray-Darling Basin agricultural zone.
The hydro reservoirs — Lake Eucumbene (the largest artificial lake in Australia),
Lake Jindabyne, and several others — are visible throughout the park.

The catchment function of the Main Range has influenced the park's management:
water quality is a priority constraint that affects what activities are
permitted in the upper catchments. The pastoral grazing that the cattlemen's
huts served was eventually prohibited — the last cattle were removed from
Kosciuszko National Park in 1969 after a Royal Commission found the grazing
was causing unacceptable catchment degradation. The huts remain as heritage
structures, but the function they served is gone.
