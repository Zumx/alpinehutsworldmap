The High Atlas of Morocco rises abruptly from the Marrakech plain to form a wall of peaks, valleys, and passes stretching over 700 kilometres from the Atlantic coast near Agadir to the Algerian border east of Figuig. The highest section — the western High Atlas between Marrakech and the Draa valley — includes Toubkal (4,167 m), the highest summit in North Africa, along with a dense cluster of peaks above 4,000 m in the Toubkal massif and the Ouanoukrim group. The central High Atlas around Mgoun (4,071 m) is lower but topographically complex, with long ridge traverses and deeply cut valleys. The overnight shelter network here mixes two distinct traditions: the gîte d'étape, a family-run or community-managed building in Berber villages, and the refuge maintained by the Club Alpin Français (CAF) — the first European alpine club to establish a systematic shelter network in Africa, from the 1930s onward. The ten shelters below cover the principal overnight stops from the Toubkal ascent route to the Mgoun traverse.

## 1. Refuge Tazaghart, Toubkal Massif

Refuge Tazaghart stands at 3,600 m on the Tazaghart plateau west of the main Toubkal summit group, positioned below the striking Tazaghart cliff — a 300-metre limestone escarpment that dominates the plateau's northern edge. The CAF refuge here is the base for the Toubkal west face approaches and for the less-visited Afella (3,914 m) and Akioud (3,677 m) peaks. It is smaller and quieter than the main Toubkal refuges, with capacity for around 30 people, and offers a better acclimatisation base for the full Toubkal circuit than the crowded Neltner refuge.

## 2. Chalet du CAF, Oukaïmeden

Chalet du CAF is located at Oukaïmeden, a ski resort at 2,600 m about 70 km south of Marrakech — the highest ski area in Africa. The chalet was established in the 1930s as the CAF's base in the western High Atlas and serves as the most accessible high-altitude lodge in the range, reachable by road from Marrakech in about two hours. It is the standard acclimatisation base for parties preparing for Toubkal and offers comfortable rooms, a proper kitchen, and a library of climbing guides for the Atlas.

## 3. Gite Entre les Vallées, Aït Benhaddou Region

Gite entre les vallées is a Berber guesthouse in the foothills of the High Atlas on the southern side of the range, serving trekkers crossing between the Draa and Dades valleys. This part of the range is lower and more arid than the Toubkal massif but scenically extraordinary — the ochre-walled kasbahs of the southern slopes give way to rocky passes and cedar forests at mid-elevation. The gite offers the standard Moroccan mountain hospitality: mint tea on arrival, a communal tagine dinner, and simple bedrooms with Berber carpets.

## 4. GITE Tizi-n-Oucheg, Toubkal Approaches

Gite Tizi-n-Oucheg is positioned below the Tizi-n-Oucheg pass at around 2,300 m on the standard approach route to Toubkal from Imlil via the Azzaden valley. This valley route is less crowded than the main Imlil-Aroumd-Neltner corridor and passes through villages where traditional Tachelhit-speaking Berber life continues largely unchanged. The gite is family-run and provides a perfect overnight stop for parties doing a two-day Toubkal ascent via the western approach.

## 5. Ruins de Refuge de Tacheddirt, Tacheddirt Valley

The refuge de Tacheddirt was an original CAF shelter at 2,314 m in the Tacheddirt valley, now largely ruined but historically important as one of the first purpose-built mountaineering shelters in Africa. The village of Tacheddirt itself remains occupied and several family gites have replaced the former CAF function. The valley is a key junction point for routes connecting Imlil with Oukaïmeden and the eastern traverses toward Mgoun. The ruins are a waypoint on the main trekking trail and have an instructive role in understanding the history of French mountaineering in North Africa.

## 6. Gite d'Aït Daoud, Mgoun Massif

Gite d'Aït Daoud is a village guesthouse in the Aït Daoud area on the northern approaches to Mgoun (4,071 m), the highest summit in the central High Atlas. The Mgoun massif is more remote than Toubkal — the nearest road approaches from Azilal or from the Aït Benhaddou direction — and the trekking routes here are longer and more committing. The gite at Aït Daoud is typically the second or third overnight stop on the classic Mgoun traverse from the Aït Bou Oulli valley to the Kelaat Mgouna valley via the summit.

## 7. Refuge du Toubkal, Toubkal Base Camp

Refuge du Toubkal at 3,207 m in the village of Aroumd (also spelled Arround) is the lower base camp for Toubkal, operated in partnership with local Berber guides. It lies about 4 km below the Neltner hut and is often used as the first overnight stop for parties arriving too late to push all the way to high camp on the first day. The refuge has a full restaurant service and comfortable multi-bed rooms. The muleteer service connecting Imlil to Aroumd is still actively used by trekkers without the fitness for the full load-carry.

## 8. Refuge Neltner, Toubkal High Camp

Refuge Neltner at 3,207 m is the primary high camp for Toubkal — technically the building sits at around 3,200 m and the ascent to the summit from the refuge door takes three to four hours by the standard south couloir route. The refuge was built by the CAF in 1935 and has been expanded and modernised multiple times since. It sleeps around 60 people in two main dormitories and generates its own electricity. In peak season (July-August) the refuge fills to capacity and advance reservation via the CAF Casablanca section is strongly recommended.

## 9. Refuge Lepiney, Toubkal Massif

Refuge Lepiney is situated at 2,900 m on the eastern side of the Toubkal massif in the Tacheddirt / N'Fiss watershed area, named after the French alpinist Jacques de Lépiney who made several first ascents in the Atlas in the 1920s. The refuge is maintained by the CAF and has capacity for around 40 people. It is less frequently used than Neltner but provides access to the eastern faces of the main peaks and is an important waypoint on the Grand Toubkal Circuit — a multi-day loop around the entire massif.

## 10. Espace Tamount, Toubkal Foothills

Espace Tamount is a modern lodge and trekking centre in the Imlil valley foothills, positioned to serve parties arriving for Toubkal acclimatisation days or returning from summit attempts. The facility has a professional guide-booking service, equipment rental, and a detailed knowledge of current conditions on the mountain — essential information given that the Toubkal summit can hold dangerous snow and ice as late as May and as early as October. The lodge also organises muleteer services for parties needing load support.
