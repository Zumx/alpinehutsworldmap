The Prokletije — the Accursed Mountains — stretch across the borders of Albania, Montenegro, and Kosovo, forming the wildest and most remote section of the Dinaric Alps. On the Albanian side, the range is centred on the Valbona and Theth valleys, separated by the Valbona Pass at around 1,800 m. The highest peak, Maja Jezercë, reaches 2,694 m and is the highest summit in the Dinaric Alps outside Bosnia. Prokletije became an Albanian national park in 2009, and the establishment of the Peaks of the Balkans trail in 2012 — a 192-kilometre loop connecting the Albanian, Montenegrin, and Kosovan sections of the range — transformed international trekking interest in the area. Before the trail existed, the Prokletije was one of Europe's most isolated mountain regions; today it sees several thousand foreign hikers per year. The accommodation here consists largely of family-run guesthouses (bujtina) rather than traditional alpine club huts, representing a distinct model of mountain hospitality rooted in Kanun law — the ancient Albanian customary code governing hospitality.

## 1. Alpin Hut, Valbona Valley

Alpin Hut is a purpose-built mountain shelter in the Valbona valley, the deeper and more dramatically sculpted of the two main Prokletije valleys on the Albanian side. Valbona is flanked by vertical limestone walls rising over 1,000 m, and the river running along the valley floor is a clear, braided channel of glacial origin. The hut serves as a base for ascents of the peaks above Valbona and for the standard crossing to Theth via the Valbona Pass. Accommodation is in dormitory and private rooms.

## 2. Bujtina Leonard, Theth

Bujtina Leonard is one of the longest-established family guesthouses in Theth, the old Shkodër district mountain village that was isolated for most of the communist period (1944–1991) by the difficulty of the road over the mountains. Theth's traditional stone tower houses, the kulla, still stand alongside the guesthouse buildings that have multiplied since the late 2000s. The bujtina has comfortable rooms and serves traditional Albanian food — fergese, tave kosi, and cornbread. The Theth National Park surrounding the village protects a cirque landscape of extraordinary beauty.

## 3. Vie Nomade, Peaks of the Balkans Trail

Vie Nomade operates as a trail hostel on the Peaks of the Balkans route in the Albanian section of the range. The guesthouse model adopted by Vie Nomade and similar operations along the trail has transformed trekking logistics — before 2012 it was difficult to plan a multi-day route with guaranteed overnight shelter; today the trail is bookable end to end. Vie Nomade is particularly focused on supporting slower-paced itineraries and offers guiding services for the higher passes.

## 4. Bujtina Zici, Valbona

Bujtina Zici is a family-run guesthouse in the Valbona area, positioned to serve hikers arriving over the pass from Theth or departing northward toward the Montenegrin border. The family has been hosting mountaineers since the early 2000s, before the Peaks of the Balkans trail formalised the route. Traditional hospitality here includes unannounced extra courses appearing at dinner and early morning packed lunches prepared before guests rise. The overnight rate typically includes dinner and breakfast.

## 5. Bujtina and Ferma Ded Nika, Theth

Bujtina and Ferma Ded Nika combines accommodation with a working farm in Theth village. Guests can observe or participate in cheese-making, wood-chopping, and the daily rhythms of a high-mountain Albanian farm still operating with traditional practices. The rooms are simple but comfortable, and the farmhouse kitchen produces some of the best meals on the Theth circuit. The farm sits at around 780 m in the Theth valley floor, surrounded by the dramatic limestone towers of the Theth national park.

## 6. Bujtina MARJAN ÇARDAKU, Northern Albania

Bujtina Marjan Çardaku is a guesthouse in the northern Prokletije area beyond Theth, serving hikers on the longer cross-border sections of the Peaks of the Balkans trail. This part of the route traverses higher and more remote terrain than the classic Valbona-Theth crossing, and the shelter at Marjan Çardaku is a welcome fixed point in an otherwise sparsely inhabited landscape. The surrounding terrain above the guesthouse offers access to some of the least-visited peaks in the entire Prokletije.

## 7. Bujtina and Restaurant Prek Bjeshka, Valbona Area

Bujtina and Restaurant Prek Bjeshka occupies a valley-floor position in the Valbona watershed and serves both day visitors arriving by jeep taxi from Bajram Curri and overnight hikers. The restaurant component is well known locally for its grilled meats and freshwater fish. Prek Bjeshka means "Across the Mountain" in Albanian, reflecting the position of the guesthouse relative to the high passes that define the Prokletije landscape. The building is a modern stone-and-timber construction with a large terrace facing the eastern valley walls.

## 8. Bujtina Gzim Stani, Valbona Valley

Bujtina Gzim Stani is one of the original family guesthouses in Valbona, predating the trail formalisation by several years. The Stani family hosted early trekking pioneers in the valley when guesthouse facilities were improvised rather than purpose-built, and the operation today reflects that long experience. The hut sits near the upper end of the accessible valley, at around 1,200 m, making it a natural staging point for the final ascent to the Valbona Pass. Accommodation includes rooms with mountain views and a terrace for post-hike recovery.

## 9. Zastan Karaula, Border Area

Zastan Karaula is an unusual shelter — a converted former military post near the Montenegro border, repurposed as a trekking shelter for Peaks of the Balkans hikers. "Karaula" means border guard post in Albanian and Serbian, and the building's institutional past gives it a distinct character unlike the family bujtina elsewhere on the route. The position provides outstanding views across the triple-border zone where Albania, Montenegro, and Kosovo meet in the highest reaches of the Prokletije.

## 10. Spiridon, Theth Area

Spiridon guesthouse is in Theth village and named for a local family patriarch. It is one of the newer additions to the Theth accommodation network, built in response to the rapid growth in visitor numbers since around 2015. The building respects the traditional stone construction style of the village while offering modern plumbing and solid beds. Spiridon is often the last overnight stop before the classic hike to the Grunasi waterfall — a 30-metre cascade hidden in a canyon above the village — and the Blue Eye spring.
