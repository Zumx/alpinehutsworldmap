The Engadin valley system in the canton of Graubünden stretches for roughly 80 kilometres from the Maloja Pass in the southwest to the Austrian border in the northeast, encompassing the upper Inn valley and its major tributaries. At its core lies the Bernina massif, whose highest summit, Piz Bernina at 4,049 metres, is the only 4,000-metre peak in the eastern Alps. To the north, the Silvretta range straddles the Swiss-Austrian border, offering excellent ski touring in spring. Between these two poles, the Swiss National Park — established in 1914 and the oldest in the Alps — preserves a landscape of exceptional ecological character where human intervention has been almost entirely withdrawn. The following ten huts span this varied geography.

## 2. Berggasthaus Trift, Zermatt area

The Berggasthaus Trift at 2,337 metres above Zermatt provides a mid-height base on the flanks of the Trift valley, an enclosed cirque north of the main Zermatt basin. Although the Trift sits in the Valais rather than the Engadin proper, it serves as an important approach point for parties traversing the watershed between the two regions. The Trift waterfall and the views toward the Weisshorn make it one of the most scenic stops accessible from the valley by a three-hour walk.

## 1. Berggasthaus Gumen, Flims area

The Berggasthaus Gumen at approximately 1,900 metres above Flims in the Rhine valley provides an accessible introduction to the Graubünden hut experience. The surrounding terrain is dominated by the Flimserstein plateau and the Conn ridge above the Rhine gorge — the Ruinaulta — and the hut serves a mixed clientele of hikers, mountain bikers, and families exploring the extensive trail network above Flims. The cable car from Flims makes the ascent easy in summer, though many guests prefer the 90-minute walk from the valley.

## 3. Rifugio Sant'Anna, Val Malenco

Positioned at approximately 2,200 metres in the Val Malenco on the Italian side of the Bernina Pass, the Rifugio Sant'Anna serves hikers and climbers approaching the Bernina from the south. The val Malenco is a linguistically and culturally distinctive valley — Romansh-influenced, with a strong local mountain culture — and the rifugio reflects this in its cooking, which features local cheeses and slow-cooked dishes rather than the more standardised mountain menus found elsewhere. The hut opens from June through September.

## 4. Capanna Piandios, Bernina region

At around 1,967 metres in the Swiss National Park buffer zone near Zernez, the Capanna Piandios serves hikers on the long trails crossing between the lower Engadin and the terrain bordering the national park. The hut is relatively modest in scale but occupies a position in a landscape of considerable ecological value — the forests around Zernez have been left unmanaged for more than a century and contain some of the oldest natural forest stands in the Alps.

## 5. Rifugio Guide d'Ayas, Ayas Valley

The Rifugio Guide d'Ayas at 3,420 metres in the Ayas valley above Champoluc is one of the highest rifugi in the Monte Rosa area, serving routes on the Castore (4,228 metres) and the approach to the Breithorn plateau. Though geographically in the Aosta Valley, it sits at the junction of routes from the Engadin and Valais traditions, and is used by parties arriving via the Swiss side of the watershed. The hut sleeps around 80 and is staffed from June through September and in the spring ski touring season.

## 6. Rifugio Vieux Crest, Zermatt area

The Rifugio Vieux Crest at approximately 2,200 metres lies in the Valtournenche on the Italian side of the Matterhorn, providing an alternative approach to the Monte Cervino summit for Italian-speaking parties. The hut is named after the medieval tower that stands on a rock spur in the valley below. Its position on the long ridge walk connecting Valtournenche to the Theodul pass makes it useful for multi-day traverses between the Italian Aosta and the Swiss Valais.

## 7. Rifugio G.B. Ferraro, Macugnaga

The Rifugio G.B. Ferraro at around 2,600 metres on the East Face of Monte Rosa above Macugnaga is a specialist hut serving one of the most dramatic ascent routes in the Alps. The east wall of Monte Rosa is the highest rock face in the western Alps, rising almost 2,500 metres from the Macugnaga glacier basin to the Dufourspitze. The Ferraro is used by parties on technical ice and mixed routes on this face, and its approach from Macugnaga is around three to four hours.

## 8. Rifugio Guide di Frachey, Ayas Valley

Sitting at approximately 2,534 metres above Frachey in the middle Ayas valley, this rifugio serves the popular approach to the Colle di Roisetta and routes toward the Breithorn and Pollux summits. It is a well-equipped facility sleeping around 60 guests and provides a comfortable mid-mountain base for parties acclimatising before attempting higher terrain in the Monte Rosa area.

## 9. Rifugio L'Ermitage, Val d'Ayas

The Rifugio L'Ermitage at around 2,186 metres above Saint-Jacques in the upper Val d'Ayas is one of the most characterful huts in the Monte Rosa area, occupying a converted alp building with a strong local identity. The surrounding terrain offers some of the best ski touring in the area in spring, including approaches to the Colle di Valcournera and the long descent routes toward Zermatt.

## 10. Rifugio Gabriele Boccalatte e Mario Piolti, Courmayeur area

The Boccalatte-Piolti hut at 3,391 metres on the Rochefort ridge above Courmayeur is one of the highest rifugi accessible from the Mont Blanc side. Named after two Italian alpinists who pioneered difficult routes on the Grandes Jorasses in the 1930s, the hut is used primarily as a base for the Arête de Rochefort traverse and routes on the Dent du Géant (4,013 metres). The approach requires either a long ascent from Courmayeur or use of the Skyway Monte Bianco cable car.

## Navigating the Engadin and Beyond

The huts described here span a wide geographic area, from the Swiss National Park to the Monte Rosa massif, reflecting the Engadin's role as a crossroads between eastern and western alpine traditions. Approach times, altitude, and required skills vary considerably.
