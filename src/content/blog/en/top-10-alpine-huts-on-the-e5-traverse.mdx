The E5 is among the most celebrated long-distance hiking routes in the Alps. Running approximately 180 kilometres from Oberstdorf in the Allgäu Alps of Bavaria to Merano in South Tyrol, it crosses the full width of the Eastern Alps in seven to ten days, passing through Austria's Zugspitze massif, the Ötztal Alps, and the Weißkamm before descending into the Mediterranean-influenced Adige valley. The route was formalised as part of the European long-distance path network in the 1970s and today attracts tens of thousands of hikers each summer, making its mid-section — particularly the huts around the Ötztal — heavily booked from mid-July through late August. The crossing involves some of the highest terrain outside technical mountaineering in the Alps: the Similaun area crests above 3,500 metres, and walkers must navigate glaciated terrain between the Vernagt and Similaun huts. Ice axe and crampons are mandatory equipment for this section from late June until the snow hardens in August; in September it can become icy again. Below are ten of the essential overnight stops along the route.

## 1. Kemptner Hütte, Allgäu Alps

Kemptner Hütte at 1,844 metres in the Allgäu Alps above Oberstdorf is the first major overnight stop on the E5. Owned by the DAV section Kempten since 1891, it was rebuilt to 90-bed capacity in the twentieth century and operates from June to October. The approach from Oberstdorf via the Oytal takes around four hours through increasingly open alpine terrain. The hut marks the first night on the traverse and provides the opportunity to assess fitness and weather before the route climbs toward the Austrian border at Mädelgabelpass the following morning.

## 2. Memminger Hütte, Lechtal Alps

At 2,242 metres in the Lechtal Alps of Austrian Tyrol, the Memminger Hütte is the second major stage hut and the first above 2,000 metres. DAV section Memmingen built the hut in 1890 and it was substantially extended in 1973. Current capacity is around 130 beds, making it one of the larger huts on the route. The position in the Parseiertal offers superb views of the Lechtal range and access to the rugged terrain of the Parseierspitze group. Stage two from Kemptner Hütte involves crossing the Mädelgabelpass at 2,645 metres before descending to the hut.

## 3. Braunschweiger Hütte, Ötztal Alps

The Braunschweiger Hütte at 2,759 metres in the Ötztal Alps is the first glacier-adjacent hut on the E5 and a significant step up in altitude. Operated by DAV section Braunschweig since 1896, it sits on a rocky spur above the Rofenache valley with direct access to the Weißkamm glaciers above. The hut sleeps 90 and provides crampons rental and glacier travel advice. Stage four from the previous valley crossing to the Braunschweiger is one of the E5's most spectacular — ascending from lowland Ötztal scenery to a perch on the edge of the Ötztal's glacial world.

## 4. Vernagthütte, Weißkamm

The Vernagthütte at 2,766 metres is the last DAV hut before the route enters the glaciated Weißkamm and approaches the Italian border. Built by DAV section Munich in 1896 and reconstructed in 1932, it sleeps 80 and is a vital staging point for the most demanding section of the E5 — the crossing of the Similaun glacier area. The hut maintains a guide service for parties requiring assistance on the glacier, and its position above the Vernagt reservoir provides a dramatic backdrop. From here, experienced parties push on to the Similaunhütte via the Hochjochhospiz on a long but technically achievable day.

## 5. Weißkamm Hütte, glacier approach

The Weißkamm Hütte operates at approximately 2,900 metres as an intermediate shelter on the glacier crossing between the Vernagthütte and the Similaunhütte. It is a smaller facility — around 30 beds — and functions primarily as a weather retreat and emergency refuge on the exposed upper glacier. Summer crevasse conditions on the Weißkamm glacier vary considerably by year; parties should obtain current conditions from the previous night's hut keeper before setting out.

## 6. Similaunhütte, South Tyrol border

The Similaunhütte at 3,019 metres straddles the Austrian-Italian border on the Similaun ridge, one of the most historically charged mountain locations in the Alps. The Ötzi the Iceman site — where a Copper Age mummy was discovered in 1991 — lies barely two kilometres to the east at 3,210 metres. The hut, operated by the South Tyrolean Mountain Club (AVS), sleeps 60 and serves as the E5's highest overnight point. Walkers emerge from the glacier zone onto the ridge with views stretching from the Ortler group in the south to the main Ötztal peaks in the north.

## 7. Martin-Busch-Hütte, Val Senales

Descending from the Similaunhütte into Italy's Val Senales (Schnalstal), walkers reach the Martin-Busch-Hütte at 2,501 metres — also known as the Nürnberger Hütte in older sources. Operated by the DAV section Nuremberg, it sleeps 100 and is the main Italian-side staging point before the route completes its descent into the Vinschgau valley. The hut was built in 1893 and takes its name from a Nuremberg mountaineer killed in the Alps in 1915. The val Senales above the hut contains one of South Tyrol's small glacier-ski areas, operating from October to May.

## 8. Hochjochhospiz, Val Senales headwall

The Hochjochhospiz at 2,412 metres in the upper Val Senales is an alternative staging point on the route down from the Weißkamm glacier. It is among the oldest mountain shelters in South Tyrol, with a hospice function dating to the medieval period when the Hochjoch pass served as a trans-Alpine trading route. The current building dates from the twentieth century and sleeps 60. Some E5 walkers use it instead of or in addition to the Similaunhütte, particularly when weather on the higher glacier route is deteriorating.

## 9. Stettiner Hütte, Val Passiria

The Stettiner Hütte at 2,875 metres above Moos in Val Passiria (Passeiertal) is reached on an alternative finish variant of the E5. It sits in a dramatic cirque on the watershed between the Ötztal Alps and the Texel Group, sleeping 80 in a facility originally built in 1898 by DAV section Stettin (now Szczecin, Poland). From the Stettiner Hütte, the route descends the Valle di Plan into the upper Passiria valley, through the town of St. Martin in Passeier — birthplace of the Tyrolean freedom fighter Andreas Hofer — and eventually to Merano.

## 10. Zwickauer Hütte, Ötztal southern approaches

The Zwickauer Hütte at 2,988 metres operates on one of the E5 variant routes south of the Ötztal main ridge. It is a smaller hut by E5 standards — around 50 beds — operated by the Austrian Alpine Club. The surrounding terrain provides access to the Gurgl valley below and several glacier routes on the southern faces of the Ötztal Alps. Walkers who choose this variant add a day to the crossing but see less-trafficked terrain and gain a different perspective on the relationship between the glacial headwalls and the broad Adige valley visible to the south.
