The Langtang region lies 50 kilometres north of Kathmandu and is the closest major trekking zone to the Nepali capital. The Langtang Valley, Gosaikunda Lake, and the Helambu circuit are the three main routes in this area, and together they form a popular circuit for trekkers who want genuine Himalayan mountain experience without the flight to Lukla required for Everest or Annapurna approaches. The valley was devastated by the April 2015 earthquake — the village of Langtang was buried by a landslide that killed nearly 300 residents and visitors — and the subsequent reconstruction has both rebuilt the trekking infrastructure and visibly changed the landscape in the middle valley. Tea houses in Langtang are typically family-run lodges offering dormitory or private room accommodation, dinner (dal bhat is standard), and breakfast. Altitude ranges from around 1,000 metres at the Helambu circuit's lower sections to over 5,000 metres at Tserko Ri and Kyanjin Ri above the Kyanjin valley. The main trekking seasons are October-November (post-monsoon, clearest skies) and March-April (pre-monsoon, rhododendron bloom).

## 1. Sherpa Lodge, Lama Hotel area

Sherpa Lodge in the Lama Hotel settlement at around 2,380 metres in the middle Langtang valley is one of the first major tea house clusters after the steep lower gorge. The Lama Hotel area is a concentration of half a dozen lodges built in a clearing where the valley briefly widens. Sherpa Lodge offers private rooms for two to four people, a common dining room, and a wood stove that provides the centre of social life on cold November evenings. The gorge section below this point — with waterfalls, subtropical forest, and the roar of the Langtang Khola — is one of the most dramatic trail approaches in Nepal.

## 2. Pumori Lodge, Lama Hotel cluster

Pumori Lodge, adjacent to the Sherpa Lodge in the Lama Hotel area, is named after the Pumori peak near Everest — a naming practice common in Nepali tea houses where famous mountain names are deployed to attract trekkers. The lodge sleeps around 20 in basic rooms and provides the standard tea house menu of dal bhat, noodles, Tibetan bread, and potatoes with egg. The combination of lodges in the Lama Hotel clearing means competition for customers, which generally keeps prices reasonable and quality competitive.

## 3. Sagarmatha Lodge, Riverside approach

Sagarmatha Lodge (Sagarmatha is the Nepali name for Everest) operates along the riverside trail in the lower-middle valley, typically used as a first-night stop for trekkers who begin at the Dhunche or Syabru Besi entry point. The lodge is a newer construction — post-2015 earthquake rebuild — with improved sanitation and solar lighting compared to the pre-earthquake structures. Elevation here is around 1,800 metres, and the lodge provides a gentle acclimatisation base before the more sustained climbing in the upper valley. The surrounding forests are home to red pandas, though sightings require patience and early morning walks.

## 4. Korchon Hut, upper Langtang valley

Korchon is a small yak-pasture settlement at around 3,100 metres where the valley begins to open from the dense forest zone. The hut here is one of the simpler facilities on the route — a stone building with tin roof sleeping eight to twelve in a communal room — and serves primarily as an emergency shelter and resting point. Stone walls around the surrounding meadows contain the yak herds that move up from the lower valley in summer. The traditional pastoral economy here remained largely intact even after the earthquake, and yak butter and cheese are produced and sold locally.

## 5. Pillichon Hut, Gosainkunda approach

On the Gosainkunda route above Dhunche, the Pillichon Hut at around 3,400 metres provides shelter on the long climb from the ridge village of Sing Gompa toward the sacred lake at 4,380 metres. The hut is a basic tea house with four to six rooms, operating seasonally from October to May outside the monsoon. The Gosainkunda lake is a Hindu pilgrimage site and the trail sees both trekkers and pilgrims, particularly around the full moon in August (Janai Purnima) when thousands of Hindu devotees make the ascent.

## 6. Annapurna Lodge, Kyanjin Gompa

Despite the name suggesting a different range, Annapurna Lodge in Kyanjin Gompa village at 3,870 metres is one of the best-appointed lodges in the Langtang valley. Kyanjin is the endpoint of the main valley walk and a base for higher excursions to Kyanjin Ri (4,773 m) and Tserko Ri (4,984 m). The lodge has private rooms, a large dining area, and a cheese factory built with Swiss development assistance in the 1950s that still produces yak cheese sold to trekkers. Post-earthquake rebuilding has made the Kyanjin lodges generally more comfortable than they were pre-2015.

## 7. Kirat Lodge, Helambu circuit, Melamchi Pul area

The Helambu circuit south of the Langtang range follows villages inhabited predominantly by the Hyolmo (Helambu Sherpa) people and accesses a different cultural and physical landscape from the high-altitude Langtang valley. Kirat Lodge in the Melamchi Pul Bazar area at around 850 metres serves as a lowland staging point for the circuit. The name Kirat references the ancient Kirat people of eastern Nepal, a cultural connection reflected in the lodge owner's family history. The circuit ascends from here through terraced farmland and rhododendron forest.

## 8. Arun Lodge, Sermathang, Helambu

Arun Lodge in Sermathang village at approximately 2,600 metres on the Helambu plateau provides a traditional highland village base for the middle section of the Helambu circuit. The village's white-washed stone houses with carved timber windows are characteristic of Hyolmo architecture. The lodge offers simple accommodation and meals; the kitchen relies on a combination of yak dung, wood, and kerosene fuel typical of high-altitude Nepali households. October visits coincide with apple and walnut harvests that supplement the menu with fresh local produce.

## 9. Irkuwa Lodge, Ghopte area

The Ghopte area at around 3,430 metres is a junction point where trails from the Helambu circuit, the Gosainkunda lakes, and the Langtang valley meet — a geographic hub marked by a small cluster of tea houses. Irkuwa Lodge is one of these, providing accommodation for trekkers navigating the connecting route between the Helambu and Langtang circuits over the Laurebina Pass (4,610 m). The crossing is the most demanding section of the combined Helambu-Gosainkunda-Langtang route and the Irkuwa Lodge provides the pre-pass overnight.

## 10. Mendo Hotel, Shermathang-Melamchi route

Mendo Hotel in the lower Helambu circuit's eastern arm offers accommodation for walkers descending toward Melamchi or Sundarijal at around 1,600 metres. The building is a stone-and-cement construction typical of roadside settlements in the Nepal middle hills, where the distinction between a guesthouse and a family home is minimal. The owners serve standard tea house fare and provide information on the trail conditions toward Kathmandu. From this elevation, the Langtang Himal peaks are invisible behind the intervening ridges, and the final day's walking to Sundarijal — the main road trailhead — is through dense forest above the Kathmandu valley rim.
