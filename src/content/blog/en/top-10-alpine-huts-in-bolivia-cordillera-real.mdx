Bolivia's Cordillera Real is the dominant range of the Andes within the country — a chain of glaciated peaks running 160 kilometres north to south along the eastern edge of the Altiplano, separating the high plateau world above 3,600 m from the deep Yungas valleys that descend toward the Amazon basin. The range contains some of the most accessible high-altitude summits in South America: Huayna Potosí (6,088 m) is reached by many climbers in two days from La Paz via a road that ascends to 4,700 m, and the normal route requires only basic glacier travel skills, making it one of the most-climbed 6,000-metre peaks in the world. Illimani (6,438 m), visible from La Paz on clear days, is more technical. Chearoco (6,127 m) and Chearoco Norte are among the less-visited summits in the middle of the range. The refugio network here is less formalised than in Ecuador or Peru — many are privately operated by guiding agencies based in La Paz, and some are improvised high camps in stone shelters or purpose-built metal huts. The altitude of even the lowest refugios (typically 4,700–5,300 m) means acclimatisation is a serious consideration for all visitors.

## 1. Refugio Huayna Potosí, Huayna Potosí Base Camp

Refugio Huayna Potosí at approximately 4,700 m is the main base camp refuge for Huayna Potosí, accessible by road from La Paz in about two hours via El Alto and the Zongo valley road. The refugio is operated by one of the main La Paz guiding agencies and offers bunk dormitory accommodation, a cook service, and equipment rental. The standard Huayna Potosí ascent spends the first night at this base camp for acclimatisation, then moves up to the high camp at 5,300 m on the second night before the summit push.

## 2. Refugio Campo Alto Roca, Illimani

Refugio Campo Alto Roca at around 5,300 m serves as the high camp for the standard Illimani ascent via the northwest face. Illimani's base camp is reached from the village of Pinaya on the southern side of La Paz, and the approach involves a significant load-carry or use of arriero (mule driver) services to bring equipment to the high camp. The summit of Illimani at 6,438 m is the highest point visible from La Paz, and on clear days the ice cap can be seen gleaming white from the city's hilltop neighbourhoods.

## 3. Refugio Nuevo Llaulini, Cordillera Real Central

Refugio Nuevo Llaulini is a recently constructed stone and metal-roofed shelter in the central Cordillera Real, serving the approach routes to the Chearoco-Chearoco Norte group and the central peaks. The central section of the Cordillera Real between Huayna Potosí and Illimani sees fewer guided parties than the main access peaks but is traversed by the classic Cordillera Real Traverse — a multi-day high-level route connecting the northern and southern anchors of the range.

## 4. Refugio Huayna Potosí Alto Campo, High Camp

Refugio Huayna Potosí Alto Campo at approximately 5,300 m is the high camp on Huayna Potosí, a basic metal-walled structure with sleeping platforms for around 20 people. The high camp is positioned below the normal glacier route up the northeast face of the mountain and is the departure point for summit attempts, typically beginning at midnight or 1 am to ensure parties reach the summit before the afternoon snow bridges soften. At this altitude — equivalent to the summit of many Himalayan trekking peaks — altitude sickness is a serious risk for insufficiently acclimatised climbers.

## 5. Cabaña Laguna Vizcachani, Northern Cordillera Real

Cabaña Laguna Vizcachani is a shelter near the Laguna Vizcachani at around 4,400 m in the northern Cordillera Real, accessible from the La Cumbre pass on the La Paz-to-Coroico road. The laguna — a high glacial lake — is the starting point for the classic Takesi trek, one of Bolivia's most celebrated Inca trail routes descending through spectacular ecological zones from the Altiplano to the Yungas. The cabaña serves both trekkers doing the Takesi route and mountaineers approaching the northern Cordillera Real peaks.

## 6. Refugio El Alto, La Paz Approaches

Refugio El Alto is a base facility near the city of El Alto, the Aymara-majority satellite city of La Paz at 4,100 m that serves as the departure point for most Cordillera Real mountaineering expeditions. The refugio provides equipment storage, pre-expedition overnight accommodation, and logistical coordination for guided parties. El Alto itself is culturally important — its Sunday market at the Villa 16 de Julio is the largest open-air market in Bolivia — and serves as an acclimatisation base for visitors arriving from sea level before moving to higher camps.

## 7. El Edén de la Escalada, Cordillera Real

El Edén de la Escalada — "The Climber's Eden" — is a private mountain house in the approaches to the Cordillera Real from the La Paz side, operated as a climbing hostel and logistics base. The facility targets recreational climbers doing their first Andean peaks and offers coaching in glacier travel, crevasse rescue, and altitude management alongside accommodation. The name reflects the enthusiasm of the founder for making high-altitude climbing accessible to a wider international audience.

## 8. Cabaña La Espartana, Illimani Approaches

Cabaña La Espartana is a shelter in the Illimani approach corridor, positioned between the village access point and the moraine camp used by parties climbing the standard route. The cabaña serves as an intermediate overnight stop that breaks the long first day from the valley floor to the glacier edge. The surrounding landscape on the southeastern slopes of Illimani is extraordinarily dramatic — deep quebradas cut through alternating strata of red sandstone and pale granite, descending toward the cloud forest zone thousands of metres below.

## 9. Max Adventures, Huayna Potosí Area

Max Adventures operates a mountain hostel and guiding centre near the Huayna Potosí base camp, one of the most well-known agencies in the La Paz mountaineering world. The facility has been hosting climbers since the late 1990s and offers everything from single-day ice-climbing introductions on the lower Zongo glacier to full guided ascents of Huayna Potosí, Chearoco, and Illimani. The hostel reputation rests on reliable guiding standards and honest assessment of client acclimatisation levels — important in a region where altitude sickness can end a summit attempt quickly.

## 10. El Pauro, Cotapata Approaches

El Pauro is a shelter and camp site near the Cotapata National Park in the transition zone between the Altiplano and the Yungas cloud forest, northwest of La Paz near the old Yungas road. The shelter serves trekkers on the multi-day routes descending from the high Cordillera into the subtropical valleys and is also used by botanists and ecologists studying the rapid vegetation change compressed into a few thousand metres of altitude. The Cotapata park boundary begins at around 3,600 m and the descent to the forest floor at 1,200 m is possible in a single day.

## Map the Cordillera Real Refugios Before You Go

The Cordillera Real refugio network is concentrated around the main access peaks but the full traverse of the range links shelters that are separated by significant altitude and difficult glacier terrain. The map is essential for understanding the approach logistics — the distance from the road to the first overnight camp varies from two hours to a full day's walking depending on which peak you are approaching. Acclimatisation before any high-altitude work is critical; most guiding agencies recommend spending at least three to four days above 3,500 m in La Paz before attempting a glaciated summit.
