The Allgäu Alps, in the far southwestern corner of Bavaria above Oberstdorf, are unlike the bare limestone of the rest of the German Alps. Here the peaks rise as steep, grass-clad pyramids of soft marl and flysch, green almost to their summits, cut by deep valleys and crowned by sharp ridges. The Heilbronner Weg, one of the most famous high routes in Germany, traverses this country between the highest summits, and the network of huts run by the Deutscher Alpenverein makes the Allgäu one of the finest hut-walking regions in the eastern Alps. Oberstdorf, the gateway, is Germany's southernmost town and the start of countless classic tours.

## Green peaks and the flysch ridges

The Allgäu owes its distinctive character to its geology. Where the central Alps are built of hard granite and the northern ranges of pale limestone, the Allgäu mixes both, with broad zones of soft, grassy flysch and marl that allow turf to grow almost to the tops of peaks like the Höfats, famous as one of the steepest grass mountains in the Alps and the haunt of bold chamois and hardy haymakers of old. This green, steep landscape, threaded with alpine flowers and crossed by airy ridges, gives the Allgäu walking a feel all its own, soft underfoot yet often dizzyingly exposed.

## The Heilbronner Weg

The Heilbronner Weg, opened in 1899, is the classic high-level traverse of the Allgäu, running along the main ridge between the Rappenseehütte and the Kemptner Hütte beneath the highest peaks of the range. Secured with ladders, fixed cables and a famous iron ladder, it crosses near the Hohes Licht and the Bockkarkopf, passing close to the Mädelegabel (2645 m), the highest summit fully in Germany. The route demands a head for heights and sure footing but no climbing, and it is one of the great rite-of-passage walks for German mountaineers, traditionally done as a multi-day hut tour.

## Rappenseehütte

The Rappenseehütte at 2091 metres is one of the largest huts in the entire Alps, beautifully set beside the little Rappensee beneath the western end of the Heilbronner Weg. With hundreds of beds, it is a great hub of Allgäu walking, the western anchor of the high route and a base for the Hohes Licht, the highest peak in the German Allgäu. The approach from Oberstdorf via the Birgsau and the Enzianhütte is long and scenic, and the hut's lakeside terrace is one of the classic gathering places of the range.

## Kemptner Hütte

The Kemptner Hütte at 1846 metres is the eastern anchor of the Heilbronner Weg, set in a high valley beneath the Kratzer and the Mädelegabel, and one of the busiest huts in the Allgäu because it also lies on the hugely popular E5 long-distance trail from Oberstdorf to Meran. Walkers crossing the Mädelejoch into the Tyrol pass through here, making it a true crossroads. Recently modernised, it is a large, welcoming hut and a base for the eastern summits of the range.

## Nebelhorn and the Edmund-Probst-Haus

The Edmund-Probst-Haus at 1932 metres sits on the Nebelhorn above Oberstdorf, the most accessible high summit of the Allgäu, reached by cable car and famous for a panorama that takes in over four hundred peaks on a clear day. The hut is a comfortable base just below the summit, popular with walkers riding up for sunrise and with ski-tourers in winter. From the Nebelhorn the Hindelanger Klettersteig, one of Germany's finest via ferratas, runs along the jagged ridge toward the Großer Daumen, a spectacular day for those with a head for heights.

## Prinz-Luitpold-Haus

The Prinz-Luitpold-Haus at 1846 metres lies in a magnificent high cirque beneath the Hochvogel (2592 m), one of the most striking and dangerous peaks of the Allgäu, whose summit is slowly splitting apart along a widening crack. A historic and atmospheric hut, it is the base for the Hochvogel and for crossings toward the Hindelanger ridge, set among grassy slopes and rocky cirques rich in alpine flora. The long approach through the Hintersteiner Tal is one of the loveliest valley walks in the range.

## Kemptner and the Mindelheimer Hütte

The Mindelheimer Hütte at 2013 metres sits high in the Kleinwalsertal area beneath the Schafalpenköpfe, at one end of the spectacular Mindelheimer Klettersteig, a classic via ferrata along a chain of rocky summits. It is a base for the via ferrata and for ridge walks above the Kleinwalsertal, the Austrian valley reached only through Germany, with its own distinctive Walser culture. The hut's position on the frontier ridge gives wide views across both the Allgäu and the Austrian Lechtal Alps.

## Fiderepasshütte and the Kleinwalsertal

The Fiderepasshütte at 2070 metres sits on the Fiderepass beneath the Hammerspitze, on the route between the Mindelheimer Hütte and the Kanzelwand above the Kleinwalsertal. A small, classically sited hut on a high col, it serves walkers traversing the frontier ridges and is a favourite for its sunset and its quiet, high-mountain feel. The Kleinwalsertal below, a pocket of Austria accessible only from the German side, adds a curious cross-border dimension to walking in this corner of the Allgäu.

## Hut culture and the long-distance trails

The Allgäu huts share the warm DAV culture of the Bavarian Alps, with Kaiserschmarrn and Kässpatzen on every table and a strong tradition of self-catering and shared dormitories. The region is the launch point for two great long-distance routes: the E5, the popular Alpine crossing from Oberstdorf over the Mädelejoch to the Tyrol and on to Meran, and the Heilbronner Weg high traverse. The huts run mainly from June to early October, with the high routes dependent on snow clearing from the ladders and cables. Because the E5 and the Heilbronner Weg draw huge numbers, reservation is essential, and the busiest huts fill weeks ahead in high summer.

## Practical notes for the Allgau huts

Oberstdorf, reached by direct train from Munich, is the hub for almost all Allgäu walking, with the Nebelhorn and Fellhorn cable cars giving quick access to the high country. Most huts are reached in three to five hours on foot, and the high routes such as the Heilbronner Weg and the Hindelanger and Mindelheimer Klettersteige demand sure footing, a head for heights and via ferrata sets. The grassy peaks can be deceptively steep and slippery when wet. DAV membership brings reduced rates and reciprocal rights, and online reservation through the huts is now the norm across the range.
