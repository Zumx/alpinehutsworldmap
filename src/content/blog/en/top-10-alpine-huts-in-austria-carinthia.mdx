Carinthia is Austria's southernmost province, a sun-trap of warm lakes and Mediterranean light tucked behind the main alpine watershed. Its mountains face south toward Slovenia and Italy, and the climate is noticeably milder than the rest of the country — the lakes around Klagenfurt warm to swimming temperature in summer, and the slopes carry a southern flora. Two great ranges define the province: the high glaciated Hohe Tauern in the north-west, sharing the Grossglockner with Salzburg and the Tyrol, and the long limestone wall of the Karawanken and Karnische Alpen along the southern frontier. The huts below, run mostly by sections of the Austrian Alpine Club, trace a province where alpine and southern worlds meet.

## 1. Klagenfurter Hütte, Karawanken

The Klagenfurter Hütte at around 1,664 metres sits beneath the Hochstuhl, the highest peak of the Karawanken at 2,237 metres, on the wooded slopes above the Bärental south of Klagenfurt. The Karawanken form the natural border with Slovenia, and from the summits above the hut the view runs deep into both countries. The hut is a popular base for the Hochstuhl ascent and for the long Karawanken ridge traverse, and its south-facing position gives it the warm, bright character typical of Carinthian mountains.

## 2. Lienzer Hütte, Schober Group

The Lienzer Hütte at around 1,977 metres lies in the Debanttal in the Schober group, a quieter corner of the Hohe Tauern National Park north of Lienz. The Schober peaks rise above 3,000 metres without carrying large glaciers, making them excellent walking and scrambling country. The hut is a comfortable base for ascents of the Petzeck and the surrounding summits, and for the trails that link northward toward the Glockner group. The Debanttal is wild and little-frequented, with a strong sense of the park's protected character.

## 3. Elberfelder Hütte, Schober Group

The Elberfelder Hütte at around 2,346 metres sits high in the Gössnitztal in the Schober group, deep within the Hohe Tauern National Park. It is a key stage on the long-distance route around the Glockner massif and a base for the higher Schober summits. From here the views extend toward the glaciers of the main Tauern crest, and the surrounding high pastures hold marmots, chamois, and the reintroduced bearded vulture, one of the conservation success stories of the national park. The hut is reached by a steady climb and sleeps around 60.

## 4. Hochalmhütte, Ankogel-Hochalm Group

In the eastern Hohe Tauern, the huts beneath the Hochalmspitze — at 3,360 metres the highest peak of the Ankogel-Hochalm group, sometimes called the Tauern Queen — serve one of the most heavily glaciated corners of Carinthia. The Gießener and Osnabrücker huts anchor the routes onto the Hochalmspitze and the surrounding ice, and the Maltatal below, famous for its waterfalls and the high alpine road, gives the main approach. This is serious mountaineering country, with glacier travel required for the main summits.

## 5. Naturfreundehütte Vinzenz Biedner, Lavanttal

The Naturfreundehütte Vinzenz Biedner sits on the Saualpe in the eastern hills of the province, gentle pastoral country far from the high glaciers. Run by the Naturfreunde, the workers' alpine movement, it is a modest, welcoming hut serving walkers on the broad grassy ridges of the Saualpe, where the views stretch across the Lavanttal toward the higher ranges. This is the soft, rolling Carinthia of summer pasture and wildflower meadow, a counterpoint to the glaciated Tauern in the north.

## 6. Techendorfer Alm, Weissensee

The Techendorfer Alm sits in the hills above the Weissensee, one of the highest large lakes in the Alps and famous for its exceptionally clear water. The pastures here, on the Gailtaler Alpen between the lake and the Drau valley, offer gentle walking with constant views over the turquoise lake far below. The alm is a working dairy and refreshment stop, serving its own produce, and the surrounding trails make easy family days in a landscape that mixes alpine pasture with the southern warmth of the lake basin.

## 7. Dr.-Steinwender-Hütte, Nockberge

In the Nockberge, the gentle rounded grass mountains of central Carinthia, huts like the Dr.-Steinwender-Hütte serve a landscape quite unlike the rest of the Alps. The Nock peaks are soft, green domes formed of ancient crystalline rock, grazed by cattle and crossed by the panoramic Nockalmstrasse. Now a biosphere reserve, the area offers some of the easiest and most pastoral high walking in Austria, with huts that lean toward Carinthian cooking — Kärntner Kasnudeln, the regional cheese dumplings, are everywhere.

## 8. Eisentalhöhe Huts, Nockalm

Along the Nockalmstrasse near the Eisentalhöhe, a cluster of alms and huts serves walkers exploring the high Nockberge biosphere reserve at around 2,000 metres. The rounded summits here — the Rosennock and Klomnock among them — are reached by easy paths through alpenrose and bilberry, and the huts offer the warm Carinthian hospitality the province is known for. It is ideal terrain for families and for those who want high views without technical difficulty, in a uniquely soft corner of the eastern Alps.

## 9. Karnische Höhenweg Huts, Carnic Alps

Along the Carnic Alps on the Italian frontier, a chain of huts serves the Karnischer Höhenweg, one of the great long-distance traverses of the eastern Alps. The route follows the watershed ridge — and the front line of the bitter First World War mountain fighting — for over 150 kilometres, with the Wolayersee Hütte beside its jewel-like lake among the most memorable stages. The huts here look south into Italy's Friuli region, and the war's tunnels and trenches are still visible along the ridge.

## 10. Villacher Alpe, Dobratsch

The Dobratsch, the great mountain rising directly above Villach, carries huts and a panoramic road that make its high meadows accessible to all. At 2,166 metres the summit gives one of the widest views in the southern Alps, stretching across the Drau valley to the Karawanken, the Julian Alps of Slovenia, and the Carnic chain. The mountain's nature park protects rich alpine flora, and the huts on its flanks make it a popular and rewarding objective. A massive medieval rockfall scar on its southern face is still clearly visible from the valley.
