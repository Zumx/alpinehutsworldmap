Refugio Frey stands on the shore of a small glacial lake at around 1,700 metres, ringed by an amphitheatre of clean granite spires that rise like a stone crown above the water. This is the climbing heart of the Nahuel Huapi National Park, a place where rock climbers from across Argentina and the world come to test themselves on the famous needles of the Catedral massif, near the city of San Carlos de Bariloche. Built of local stone and timber, the refuge is small, atmospheric and run by the Club Andino Bariloche, and for many it is the most beautiful mountain hut in Patagonia.

## A refuge among the needles

The hut sits in a cirque beneath the spires of Cerro Catedral, a cluster of slender granite towers and walls that give the place its alpine character. The rock here is excellent, and routes of every grade rise straight from the doorstep, which is why Frey has become the spiritual home of Argentine rock climbing. The setting is intimate rather than vast: the lake, the refuge, the encircling needles and the scree slopes between them form a self-contained world that climbers happily inhabit for days at a time. Officially named for the pioneering mountaineer Emilio Frey, the refuge carries his memory into one of the country's finest climbing grounds.

## The walk in

Most visitors reach Frey on foot from the base of the Cerro Catedral ski area above Bariloche, following a well-trodden trail that contours through forest and along hillsides with views over the great Lago Nahuel Huapi. The classic approach takes roughly four hours and gains height gradually, passing through lenga forest before breaking out into the rockier upper basin near the lake. An alternative path climbs from Villa Los Coihues on the lakeshore. Either way the arrival is the same: the spires reveal themselves, the lake appears, and the little stone hut comes into view beside the water.

## Rock climbing capital of Argentina

Frey is, above all, a climbers' refuge. The granite towers offer hundreds of routes, from accessible single pitches to demanding multi-pitch lines and aretes that draw experienced alpinists. Names like the Aguja Frey and the surrounding agujas are part of the vocabulary of Argentine climbing, and the refuge buzzes through the season with the talk of routes attempted, sent and dreamed of. Even non-climbers feel the energy of the place; the cirque is a natural amphitheatre, and watching parties move on the rock above the lake from the refuge terrace is entertainment enough.

## Part of the Bariloche refuge circuit

Frey is one node in a network of mountain huts maintained by the Club Andino Bariloche, and many walkers link it into a multi-day traverse. From Frey a high route crosses passes toward Refugio San Martin, also known as Refugio Jakob, set by its own lake deeper in the range, and the circuit can be extended to the Refugio Italia at Laguna Negra, to the Refugio López on its ridge above Nahuel Huapi, and further afield to the Refugio Hielo Azul and the Cajón del Azul near El Bolsón. These traverses, crossing scree, snowfields and rocky cols, are among the great hut-to-hut journeys of northern Patagonia.

## Life at the hut

Run by the Club Andino Bariloche, Refugio Frey is a working mountain refuge with bunk space, simple hot meals, and the warm, slightly chaotic atmosphere of a place where everyone has come for the mountains. It is busiest in the southern-hemisphere summer, roughly from December to March, when the rock is dry and the days long, though it can also see snow and storms even in summer, as Patagonian weather is famously fickle. Beds are limited and the hut fills quickly in peak season, so reserving ahead through the Club Andino is wise, and carrying some flexibility in your plans is wiser still.

## Weather, water and respect

Patagonia does not hand out its summits cheaply. The weather around the Catedral needles can swing from warm sunshine to driving sleet within hours, and climbers learn to start early and watch the sky. The cirque holds its own small glacier and snow patches into the season, and the lake provides water, but the environment is fragile and heavily used. Because the whole area lies within the Nahuel Huapi National Park, Argentina's oldest, visitors are asked to camp only in designated spots, carry out all rubbish and tread lightly on the thin alpine soils that take so long to recover at this latitude and altitude.

## Why climbers fall for it

There is a particular magic to Refugio Frey that keeps drawing people back. It is the combination of world-class granite, a jewel of a lake, a cosy stone hut and the camaraderie of a climbing community gathered in one tight cirque. To sit on the terrace at dusk, watching the last light fade on the spires while the lake turns to glass, is to understand why generations of Argentine climbers regard this as hallowed ground. Whether you come to climb the needles, walk the refuge circuit or simply absorb the setting, Frey rewards the effort of getting there many times over.
