The Dolomites host one of the densest and most dramatically positioned rifugio
networks in the Alps. The rock is pale yellow-white limestone that turns orange
at sunset — a phenomenon the Ladins call enrosadüra — and the huts are often
built directly onto or against the rock faces, giving them a visual drama
matched nowhere else in the Alps. The Club Alpino Italiano (CAI) manages the
majority of rifugi through its local sections in Bolzano, Trento, Belluno, and
Cortina. Most are staffed from late June to late September. All ten are on
the [map](/map).

## 1. Rifugio Lagazuoi, 2752 m — Lagazuoi, Ampezzano Dolomites

At the top station of the Lagazuoi cable car above Passo Falzarego, the
Rifugio Lagazuoi occupies the summit plateau of the Piccolo Lagazuoi. The
cable car access makes it the most easily reached high rifugio in the
Dolomites, but the terrain around it — via ferratas descending the Lagazuoi
tunnel and cliff systems from the First World War — is genuinely demanding.
The Great War tunnel system (Gallerie della Prima Guerra Mondiale) is accessible
from the hut; guided tours through the wartime galleries are offered in season.
Capacity around 85, CAI-Cortina affiliated, full half-board.

## 2. Rifugio Auronzo, 2320 m — Tre Cime di Lavaredo, Cadore Dolomites

At the end of a paved toll road from Misurina, the Auronzo is the base for
the most photographed hike in the Dolomites — the circuit of the Tre Cime di
Lavaredo (Drei Zinnen). This accessibility means the Tre Cime circuit can be
walked by most fit hikers, and in summer the numbers show it. The best strategy
is an early morning start (before 08:00) or an overnight stay at the hut to
walk the circuit at dawn before the day visitors arrive. Capacity over 100.

## 3. Rifugio Locatelli, 2405 m — Tre Cime, Sexten Dolomites

On the north side of the Tre Cime in the Sesto/Sexten Dolomites, reached from
the Auronzo side or from Innichen/San Candido in Austria via the Carinthia-side
trails. The north face view of the Tre Cime from the Locatelli terrace —
directly below the vertical walls of the Cima Grande — is the classic
Dolomites image. Capacity around 80. The hut is managed by the CAI Sexten
section and is on the junction of the Alta Via 4 and the Alta Via 5 long-
distance routes.

## 4. Rifugio Vajolet, 2243 m — Catinaccio (Rosengarten), Fassa Valley

In the Rosengarten group above Vigo di Fassa, the Vajolet is the gateway to the
Vajolet Towers (Torri del Vajolet) — three free-standing rock spires that have
been a centre of Dolomite rock climbing since the 1880s. Reached from Pera di
Fassa via chairlift and foot in about 2 hours. Capacity around 80. The
adjacent Rifugio Preuss (2243 m) and Rifugio Re Alberto are in the same basin.
Routes on the towers range from Moderate to TD; most require a guide.

## 5. Rifugio Pordoi, 2950 m — Passo Pordoi, Sella Group

At the summit of the Pordoi cable car above the Passo Pordoi, the highest
paved pass in the Dolomites at 2239 m, the Rifugio Pordoi offers the most
direct access to the Sella plateau (Sella Hochfläche) above. At 2950 m with
cable car access, it serves as an orientation point for the Sella circuit and
an access hub for the Via Ferrata Brigata Tridentina and the approach to
Piz Boè (3152 m) — the highest point in the Dolomites. Capacity around 70.

## 6. Rifugio Boè, 2871 m — Sella Group

On the Sella plateau near Piz Boè (3152 m), the Rifugio Boè is the highest
staffed hut on the Sella massif and the main overnight option for parties
traversing the plateau or descending from Piz Boè on multi-day routes. The
plateau is a high, exposed, flat landscape — unusual in the Dolomites —
surrounded by the sharp limestone cliffs that characterize the range. Strong
afternoon thunderstorms are frequent and the plateau offers no shelter.
Capacity around 50.

## 7. Rifugio Tissi, 2262 m — Civetta Group, Zoldo Valley

On the flank of the Civetta (3220 m) — the Wall of the Dolomites — above
Palafavera in the Zoldo valley, Rifugio Tissi sits on the approach to the
Civetta northwest face. The Civetta's northwest wall is one of the great rock
faces in the Alps: 1200 m of compact vertical limestone. The rifugio serves
the approach hikes and easier via ferratas on the lower flanks, as well as
the starting point for the serious Civetta wall routes. CAI Belluno section,
capacity around 60.

## 8. Rifugio Antermoia, 2496 m — Catinaccio, Fassa Valley

Deep in the Catinaccio group above the Antermoiatal, this rifugio is one of
the more remote in the Dolomites — no cable car access, no road. Reached from
Vigo di Fassa in 3–4 hours via the Passo Antermoia trail. Capacity around 60.
The Antermoia lake below the hut is a tarn of extraordinary colour, typical
of Dolomite limestone drainage. The rifugio serves the southern Catinaccio
routes and is a waypoint on the Alta Via 2.

## 9. Rifugio Tuckett, 2272 m — Brenta Dolomites, Trentino

In the Brenta Dolomites west of the main Dolomite block, the Rifugio Tuckett
(named for Francis Tuckett, the Victorian-era British Alpine Club explorer)
serves as base for the Brenta via ferratas — among the best in the Alps —
including the Via Ferrata Bocchette and the Sentiero delle Bocchette Alte.
Reached from Madonna di Campiglio by cable car and 1-hour walk. The Brenta
are technically distinct from the eastern Dolomites — closer to the central
Alps geologically and with a more serious alpine character. Capacity around 80.

## 10. Rifugio Demetz, 2681 m — Sassolungo, Alpe di Siusi

On the flanks of the Sassolungo (Langkofel, 3181 m) above the Alpe di Siusi —
the largest high-altitude alpine meadow in Europe — the Rifugio Demetz serves
the via ferrata and climbing routes on Sassolungo and Sassopiatto. The Alpe
di Siusi is accessible by cable car from Siusi and by a plateau road (motor
vehicles restricted). The combination of meadow approach and sharp limestone
towers characterizes the western Dolomites at their best.

## Dolomites hut planning

Most rifugi accept bookings by phone or email through their CAI section
contacts. The Dolomites peak season (July–August) fills huts on popular routes
— Tre Cime, Alta Via 1, Alta Via 2 — weeks in advance. Weather in the Dolomites
is dominated by afternoon convective thunderstorms from June through September.
Aim to be in the rifugio by 13:30 to 14:00 on any day with building cloud.

## The Enrosadüra phenomenon and Ladin culture

The Dolomites take their name from the French geologist Déodat de Dolomieu,
who first described the unusual rock chemistry in 1791. The pale magnesium-
rich limestone (dolomite) reacts differently to sunset light than ordinary
limestone, producing the enrosadüra — a deep orange-pink alpenglow that
illuminates the towers and walls of the range in the fifteen minutes after
sunset. Arriving at a Dolomite rifugio in time for the enrosadüra — sitting
on the terrace with a glass of Alto Adige Gewürztraminer — is one of the
specific mountain pleasures the Alps provide.

The Dolomites are also the homeland of the Ladin people — a small Romance-
language-speaking minority of approximately 20,000 people in the Fassa, Badia,
and Gardena valleys. The Ladin language and culture are distinct from both
Italian and German; the valley villages have maintained their cultural identity
across the Austrian-to-Italian transition of 1919. Many Dolomite rifugio
guardians are Ladin-speaking locals whose family connection to the mountains
stretches back generations.

## The Alta Via routes as Dolomites structure

For anyone planning more than a weekend in the Dolomites, the Alta Via network
provides the structural framework. Alta Via 1 (Lago di Braies to Belluno,
approximately 10 days) traverses the northern Dolomites from east to west,
passing through the Tre Cime area, the Fanes plateau, and the Civetta range.
Alta Via 2 (Bressanone/Brixen to Feltre, approximately 8–10 days) is the more
demanding southern parallel, involving via ferrata sections in the Marmolada
and Pale di San Martino areas.

Both routes link the rifugi listed above and provide a structured multi-day
itinerary across the range. Book the rifugi on both routes at least 6 weeks
in advance for July and August — the Alta Via dates fill in April.
