The Tour du Mont Blanc is one of the most celebrated long-distance mountain
routes in the world: 170 km, approximately 10,000 m of accumulated elevation
gain, crossing through France, Italy, and Switzerland in a roughly 10–12 day
circuit. The accommodation ranges from village hotels and camping to high
mountain refuges and rifugi above 2000 m. The huts below are the ones that
define the alpine character of the route — the nights you remember because of
the position, the food, or the approach. All are visible on the [map](/map).

## 1. Rifugio Elisabetta, 2195 m — Val Veny, Aosta Valley

Often cited as the finest evening on the TMB, the Rifugio Elisabetta sits
above the Val Veny below the Miage Glacier with a direct view of the entire
southwest face of Mont Blanc. The approach from Courmayeur via Les Chapieux
or directly from the Val Veny road takes 2.5–4 hours depending on start point.
The kitchen has a strong reputation for Valdostana cooking — polenta, fontina-
based dishes, and the best house wine of the Italian section. CAI Courmayeur
affiliated, capacity around 100.

## 2. Rifugio Bonatti, 2025 m — Val Ferret, Aosta Valley

In the upper Italian Val Ferret above the Grand Col Ferret pass, the Rifugio
Bonatti was named for Walter Bonatti — one of the greatest alpinists of the
20th century — in 2009. At 2025 m it sits on one of the most beautiful stretches
of the entire TMB, with open views to the main range and a route that traverses
high above the valley floor. CAI Valle d'Aosta section, capacity around 80,
full half-board. Reservations in July–August fill in March and April.

## 3. Rifugio Bertone, 1989 m — Courmayeur side, Val Ferret

Above Courmayeur on the hillside approaching the TMB's Italian sector from
town, the Rifugio Bertone sits at a panoramic position with views back toward
the Grandes Jorasses north face. Reached from Courmayeur in about 2 hours.
This is typically a rest-day lunch stop or the first overnight after the
Grandes Jorasses traverse stage. Capacity around 60.

## 4. Refuge des Mottets, 1870 m — Les Chapieux, Savoie

The last French overnight before the Grand Col Ferret and the descent into
Italy. The Refuge des Mottets sits in a remote upper valley position with no
road access, which gives it a genuinely isolated character despite the large
number of TMB walkers passing through. Capacity around 60, FFCAM-affiliated,
with a strong tradition of home-cooked Savoyard food. The approach from
Les Chapieux takes about 1.5 hours on clear trail.

## 5. Refuge du Col de la Croix du Bonhomme, 2443 m — Beaufortain, Savoie

At the high point of the TMB's southeastern loop, above the village of
Les Contamines, this refuge sits at 2443 m at one of the most exposed positions
on the route. The approach from Les Contamines via the Notre-Dame de la Gorge
chapel and the Bonhomme valley is a full-day stage of 6–7 hours. Capacity
around 120, FFCAM-affiliated. The descent from here toward Bourg Saint Maurice
via Cormet de Roselend is an alternative route option on the TMB.

## 6. Refuge de la Croix du Bonhomme, 2433 m — Beaufortain

Adjacent to and sometimes confused with the Col de la Croix du Bonhomme refuge,
this hut covers overflow and provides an alternative sleeping option on the
busiest stage of the French TMB. The two refuges together hold around 160
people — which can still be insufficient in peak July weeks. Book both
options early and have a confirmed reservation before committing to this stage.

## 7. Refuge Robert Blanc, 2750 m — Beaufortain, highest point of standard TMB

The highest TMB refuge on the standard route, at 2750 m above Bourg Saint
Maurice in the Beaufortain. The approach is steep and the position exposed,
but the view of the Mont Blanc massif from this angle — looking northeast —
is one of the best panoramic positions on the route. Capacity around 60.

## 8. Refuge de Lac de Bellachat, 2152 m — Les Houches, Savoy

On the high balcony above Les Houches on the TMB's final approach to
Chamonix, the Bellachat sits at 2152 m with a direct view of the Mont Blanc
chain that concludes the circuit. This is typically the penultimate TMB
overnight before the descent to Chamonix via the Brévent ridge. Capacity
around 50, with a strong position on the route logic.

## 9. Refuge des Prés, 1860 m — Vallorcine, Chamonix Valley

In the Vallorcine section of the TMB's final Swiss-to-French section, the
Refuge des Prés provides a mid-stage overnight between the Col de Balme
and Chamonix, avoiding a long single stage into town. Capacity around 40.

## 10. Champex-Lac Area Options — Valais, Switzerland

Champex-Lac (1466 m) is the main village overnight on the Swiss sector,
sitting above the Orsieres valley. The village has multiple accommodation
options including the Auberge de la Forêt and several alpine club huts on
the surrounding plateau. The Fenêtre d'Arpette variant — bypassing Champex
via a demanding high pass — is one of the hardest and most rewarding TMB
variants, descending to Trient after crossing rocky terrain above 2665 m.

## TMB booking realities

The TMB is one of the most heavily booked routes in the Alps. July and August
reservations should be confirmed by March or April. Cancellation policies
vary; the weather-clause is your main protection if a named storm forces a
stage to change. The July–August peak season has 20,000+ walkers completing the
route annually, and hut capacity has not grown proportionally. The shoulder
season (mid-June and September) offers the same terrain with a fraction of
the logistical pressure.
