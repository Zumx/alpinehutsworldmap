## What Counts as a Staffed Hut

The distinction between a staffed mountain hut and a fixed camp, a research station, or a bivouac box is worth establishing. For this list, a staffed hut is a building where paying overnight guests sleep, where at minimum basic food and hot drinks are prepared by a resident warden, and where the structure is a permanent fixture rather than a seasonal tent installation. Research stations operated by institutions and accessible only to staff do not qualify. Seasonal high camps established for guided ascents, where guests sleep in expedition tents rather than a building, are excluded.

Altitude is measured to the hut floor or entrance threshold, not to the summit of the massif the hut sits on. Some of the most famous huts in mountaineering history qualify on this basis; others, better known by reputation than by altitude, fall below the threshold.

## 1. Capanna Margherita, Monte Rosa — 4554 m

The Capanna Margherita on the Punta Gnifetti summit of Monte Rosa is the highest staffed hut in the Alps and the highest staffed hut in Europe. Built in 1893 by the Italian Alpine Club (CAI), it has operated continuously since, initially as a meteorological and astronomical research station and subsequently as a climbing refuge. The hut accommodates approximately 70 guests in its current configuration and is staffed from late June to early September. Reaching it requires glacier travel and is graded as a PD alpine route from the most common approach via the Rifugio Gnifetti (3647 m). The altitude means that guests should allow a full acclimatisation day below before attempting an overnight stay.

## 2. Refugio Atacama, Chile — approximately 5200 m

South America hosts several high-altitude establishments serving mountaineers on Aconcagua and the high volcanoes of the Atacama. Some installations near the Atacama border operate seasonally with a warden. The exact status of structures in this region fluctuates with the guiding industry. Mule-supported camps and warden-staffed stone huts at altitudes above 5000 metres exist for Aconcagua (6961 m) approaches, though the Aconcagua base camp at Plaza de Mulas (4300 m) is more reliably staffed.

## 3. Everest Base Camp Lodges, Nepal — 5364 m

The tea houses at and near Everest Base Camp in the Khumbu valley operate as commercial lodges during the pre-monsoon season (March to May) and the post-monsoon season (September to November). Gorak Shep at 5164 metres has two lodges that serve food and provide dormitory beds. The facilities are basic and the nights are cold, but they represent genuine staffed overnight accommodation at altitude available to trekkers without mountaineering permits. Some facilities operate at or slightly above 5300 metres for the brief peak-season months.

## 4. Rifugio Carrel, Matterhorn — 3829 m

The Rifugio Carrel, also known as the Capanna J. A. Carrel or the Leone Hut, sits on the Italian ridge of the Matterhorn at 3829 metres. It is the standard overnight stop for climbers attempting the Matterhorn via the Italian (Lion) Ridge. The hut is staffed mid-July through mid-September by the Guides of Breuil-Cervinia. The approach from Breuil-Cervinia via the Testa del Leone involves sections of via ferrata. Beds in the small dormitory are in high demand during the season and reservation is essential.

## 5. Hörnlihütte, Matterhorn — 3260 m

The Hörnlihütte on the Swiss northeast ridge of the Matterhorn is the most famous approach hut in Alpine history. It houses over 160 beds and operates from July to mid-September. Its position at 3260 metres is modest by high-altitude standards, but the hut's role as the overnight stop for the majority of Matterhorn ascent attempts makes it a landmark in European mountaineering. Reservations fill within hours of opening in spring each year.

## 6. Refuge du Goûter, Mont Blanc — 3835 m

The Refuge du Goûter on the northwest ridge of Mont Blanc (4808 m) at 3835 metres is the highest staffed hut in France and the standard overnight stop for the Voie Normale ascent of Mont Blanc. The current building, opened in 2013, replaced a 1961 structure and accommodates 120 people. Staffed from mid-June to mid-September. Reaching it requires crossing the Grand Couloir, a rock and icefall gully that carries a serious objective hazard from falling stones — generally crossed before 7 am.

## 7. Cabane des Vignettes, Haute Route — 3157 m

On the classic Chamonix-Zermatt Haute Route, the Cabane des Vignettes (3157 m) is a SAC hut in the Valais that provides one of the most spectacular overnight settings in the Alps — a view across the Pigne d'Arolla, Mont Blanc de Cheilon, and the Zinalrothorn. It is staffed mid-March through mid-May for ski touring and mid-July through mid-September for summer trekking.

## 8. Ishinca Base Camp Hut, Peru — 4350 m

In the Cordillera Blanca north of Huaraz, the staffed facilities at Ishinca Base Camp represent some of the highest permanently staffed overnight lodges in the Andes accessible to trekkers without technical mountaineering equipment. The town of Huaraz itself sits at 3052 metres, making acclimatisation straightforward before approaching the high-altitude huts.

## 9. Muztagh Ata Base Camp Lodge, China — 4500 m

On the Chinese-Kyrgyz border, Muztagh Ata (7546 m) is a non-technical high-altitude peak accessible via its normal route without advanced technical skills, and Chinese guiding companies operate staffed base camps at approximately 4500 metres with fixed tent-and-hut combinations. The facilities have grown more permanent in recent years.

## 10. Simien Lodge, Ethiopia — 3260 m

The Simien Lodge in the Simien Mountains National Park, Ethiopia, is the highest fully staffed lodge in Africa at approximately 3260 metres. It provides hotel-standard accommodation for trekkers in the Simien Mountains, a region of dramatic escarpments and endemic wildlife including the Gelada baboon and Ethiopian wolf.
