The Rwenzori Mountains on the border of Uganda and the Democratic Republic of Congo are one of Africa's most remarkable and least-visited mountain environments. Unlike Kilimanjaro or Mount Kenya, which are isolated volcanic peaks rising from surrounding plains, the Rwenzori is a fault-block range approximately 120 kilometres long and 50 kilometres wide, raised by geological uplift rather than volcanic activity. The highest summit, Margherita Peak on Mount Stanley, reaches 5,109 m and carries one of the last surviving tropical glaciers in Africa — glaciers that have retreated dramatically since the late nineteenth century but still cling to the highest massifs. The range was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1994 and is entirely within Rwenzori Mountains National Park, managed by the Uganda Wildlife Authority. The Rwenzori circuit — a standard eight-day route through the range from the Nyakalengija trailhead near Kasese — passes through an extraordinary ecological sequence: montane forest, bamboo, giant heather, Lobelia giant groundsel (the famous "surreal landscape" of enormous alien-looking plants), and finally the moraine and ice zone. The hut network along the circuit was rebuilt and improved in the 2000s by the Rwenzori Mountaineering Services (RMS) after damage from flooding and the 1997–2000 conflict period.

## 1. Nyabitaba Hut, Lower Forest Zone

Nyabitaba Hut at 2,651 m is the first overnight stop on the standard Rwenzori circuit, reached in four to five hours from the Nyakalengija trailhead near Kasese. The trail climbs through montane forest of Podocarpus and Macaranga, with colobus monkeys frequently seen in the canopy. The hut has dormitory accommodation for around 30 people and is managed by RMS staff who also maintain the trails. The Mubuku river crossing below the hut can be challenging in heavy rain.

## 2. John Matte Hut, Upper Forest

John Matte Hut at 3,414 m is the second overnight stop on the main circuit, reached from Nyabitaba in four to six hours through the transition from forest to bamboo and then into the lower Lobelia zone. The hut is named after a long-serving Rwenzori guide and naturalist. At this elevation the vegetation begins its characteristic transition into the giant forms — Lobelia wollastonii plants with 3-metre flowering spikes, Dendrosenecio groundsels with cabbage-like rosettes on two-metre stalks — that make the Rwenzori unlike any other mountain environment on Earth.

## 3. Bujuku Hut, Central Rwenzori

Bujuku Hut at 3,962 m is the central overnight stop of the circuit, positioned beside Bujuku Lake in the heart of the range between Mount Stanley to the west and Mount Speke and Mount Baker to the east. The location is among the most atmospheric on the entire circuit — the lake reflects the surrounding peaks when the frequent mist and cloud clear, and the giant groundsels (some 10 metres tall) tower over the hut. The hut accommodates around 20 people and is the base for acclimatisation climbs on Mount Speke and the start of the Elena Glacier approach to Margherita Peak.

## 4. Guy Yeoman Hut, Bujuku Valley

Guy Yeoman Hut at around 4,060 m is a higher camp in the Bujuku valley above the main Bujuku Hut, positioned closer to the Elena Glacier approach on Mount Stanley. The hut is named after the British botanist and conservationist Guy Yeoman, who made important contributions to Rwenzori ecology. It is used primarily by technical climbing parties attempting the glacier routes and by trekkers acclimatising before the Kitandara crossing.

## 5. Kitandara Hut, Southern Circuit

Kitandara Hut at 4,028 m sits beside the two Kitandara Lakes on the southern section of the circuit, below Mount Luigi di Savoia. The lakes are still, dark, and extraordinarily beautiful — surrounded by giant groundsels and backed by rocky peaks. From Bujuku the crossing to Kitandara via the Scott Elliot Pass (4,372 m) is the most dramatic day on the circuit, crossing the watershed of the range with views in both directions over the major glacier valleys. The hut accommodates around 20 people.

## 6. Bamwanjara Hut, Northern Circuit

Bamwanjara Hut at approximately 3,280 m is on the northern section of the circuit connecting the main route back toward Nyakalengija or toward the Freshfield Pass and the northern approaches. The hut is positioned in the upper Heather zone where tree heather (Erica arborescens) grows to five metres or more, draped in old-man's beard lichen, creating a fairy-tale tunnel effect on the trail. The RMS-maintained hut system on the northern circuit is less visited than the main Bujuku loop.

## 7. Hunwick's Camp, Nyabitaba Area

Hunwick's Camp is a clearing and camp site in the forest zone below Nyabitaba, used as an alternative first-night stop by parties with late afternoon starts from the trailhead. The surrounding forest holds blue monkeys, black-and-white colobus, and the African olive pigeon, and the air is humid and rich with the smell of moss. Hunwick's Camp represents the lowest overnight option in the RMS network, making it accessible for less fit hikers who prefer a shorter first day.

## 8. Bugata Camp, Upper Mubuku Valley

Bugata Camp is positioned in the upper Mubuku valley at around 3,500 m, on the approach route from Nyabitaba toward the high circuit via the direct Mubuku valley approach. This route is an alternative to the standard path via John Matte Hut and provides a slightly different perspective on the central range. The camp has basic shelter and is used by RMS-organised expeditions on custom itineraries rather than the standard eight-day circuit.

## 9. Kalalama Camp, Northern Approaches

Kalalama Camp is on the northern circuit of the Rwenzori, above the Kalalama valley that drains northward from the central peaks. This section of the range is the least visited and requires local guide knowledge to navigate confidently — the trails are less worn and the vegetation dense. The camp serves as a base for exploring the northern massifs including the peaks of Mount Emin and Mount Gessi, which are rarely climbed and offer a genuine sense of remote expedition mountaineering.

## 10. Nyamwamba Hut, Southern Circuit

Nyamwamba Hut is on the southern section of the Rwenzori circuit above the Nyamwamba valley, which drains eastward from the central peaks toward Kasese. The hut is on the longer southern approach and provides shelter for parties extending their circuit beyond the standard Bujuku-Kitandara loop. The Nyamwamba valley below the hut contains impressive waterfalls in the wet season and a rich bird fauna including the Rwenzori turaco, a brilliantly coloured green and red endemic species found only in this mountain range.

## Plan Your Rwenzori Circuit on the Map

The Rwenzori circuit requires advance booking through Rwenzori Mountaineering Services or Uganda Wildlife Authority, and the hut positions relative to each other are critical for building a realistic day-by-day itinerary. [Open the map](/map) to see the full circuit plotted on the terrain, with altitude contours showing the two major passes — Scott Elliot and Freshfield — that are the most demanding sections of each day's walk. The standard eight-day circuit is the minimum for an acclimatisation approach to Margherita Peak; parties attempting the summit should plan ten to twelve days. The driest months — January-February and June-August — give the best summit conditions, though some cloud and rain is normal year-round due to the equatorial location.
