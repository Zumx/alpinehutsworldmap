Mount Kenya (5,199 m) is Africa's second-highest mountain and the most topographically complex — unlike Kilimanjaro's broad shield or the Rwenzori's cloud forest ridges, Mount Kenya is a shattered volcanic plug with twin technical peaks (Batian and Nelion, both requiring technical rock climbing), a series of subsidiary summits accessible to strong hikers, and a radial pattern of forested ridges and glacial valleys descending in all directions. The mountain lies almost exactly on the equator in central Kenya, and its climate zones stack vertically from equatorial rainforest through bamboo, Hagenia woodland, moorland with giant lobelia and senecio groundsels, alpine desert, and finally permanent snow and ice above 4,800 m. The mountain has been a Kenya Wildlife Service national park since 1949, a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1997, and a Biosphere Reserve since 1978. The accommodation network reflects this scale: Kenya Wildlife Service bandas (simple furnished huts) in the forest zone, large trekking huts at the moorland edges, and high camps in the alpine zone serving both peak baggers on Point Lenana (4,985 m) and technical climbing parties on the main peaks.

## 1. Mackinder's Hut, Teleki Valley

Mackinder's Hut (also called Teleki Valley Hut) at 4,300 m is the principal high camp on the Naro Moru route — the most popular approach to Mount Kenya, ascending through the forest and the "Vertical Bog" on the west side of the mountain to the Teleki valley below the main peaks. The hut is named after the geographer Sir Halford Mackinder, who made the first ascent of Batian in 1899 with guides Cesar Ollier and Joseph Brocherel. Capacity is around 60 people in dormitories, managed by the Mountain Rock group. From here, Point Lenana is reachable in two to three hours; the Austrian Hut is one hour above.

## 2. Shipton's Camp, Sirimon Route

Shipton's Camp at 4,200 m on the Sirimon route (the northern approach to the mountain) is named after Eric Shipton, who made important first ascents on Mount Kenya in the 1920s and 1930s. The camp is a collection of stone and timber huts with dormitory accommodation and a small dining area. The Sirimon approach through open moorland is considered less arduous than the Naro Moru "Vertical Bog" and gives better views of the main peaks during the ascent. The camp is the standard second night on a four- to five-day Sirimon circuit.

## 3. Old Moses Camp, Sirimon Gate

Old Moses Camp at 3,300 m is the first overnight stop on the Sirimon route, positioned at the forest-moorland transition zone. The camp has several large bandas with bunk beds and a cook shelter, and is run by the Kenya Wildlife Service. The surrounding moorland at this elevation is covered in Erica arborescens shrubs and giant lobelias — the tree-sized Lobelia telekii reaches several metres tall. The nearby streams are clear and cold, draining directly from the upper snowfields.

## 4. Met Station, Naro Moru Route

The Met (Meteorological) Station at 3,050 m on the Naro Moru route is a Kenya Wildlife Service facility originally built to support the mountain weather monitoring network. It now serves primarily as the first overnight stop for hikers on the Naro Moru approach, with basic banda accommodation and camping facilities. The forest around the station is a transition zone between highland forest with cedar and Hagenia, and the upper bamboo belt that characterises the slopes between 2,500 and 3,000 m.

## 5. Austrian Hut, Summit Area

Austrian Hut at 4,790 m — the highest hut on Mount Kenya — sits directly below the main peaks of Batian, Nelion, and Point Lenana, at the foot of the Lewis Glacier. Built by the Austrian Alpine Club in the 1970s, it is now managed by the Mountain Rock group and sleeps around 20 people in basic dormitory conditions. Oxygen saturation at this altitude is approximately 60% of sea-level values, and altitude sickness is the primary risk for hikers who have ascended too quickly. Point Lenana is 45 minutes from the hut on a standard morning ascent for sunrise.

## 6. Tusk Camp, Chogoria Route

Tusk Camp is on the Chogoria route — the eastern approach to Mount Kenya, widely considered the most scenic — above the Meru-side forest. The Chogoria route passes through extraordinary giant groundsel forests on its upper section and approaches the summit plateau via the dramatic Hall Tarns at around 4,300 m. Tusk Camp provides basic overnight accommodation at mid-elevation on the route and is the first stop after leaving the Chogoria forest station and crossing the moorland.

## 7. Meru Mount Kenya Bandas, Meru Side

The Meru Mount Kenya Bandas are a set of Kenya Wildlife Service overnight facilities on the Meru (eastern) approaches to the mountain, serving hikers beginning the Chogoria route from the Meru side. The bandas are simple timber structures with basic furnishings, set in the montane forest zone at around 2,000 to 2,500 m. The Meru approach is longer than the Naro Moru or Sirimon routes but passes through arguably the most diverse forest on the mountain, with good birdwatching opportunities.

## 8. Meru Bandas, Upper Chogoria

The upper Meru Bandas are positioned higher on the Chogoria route at around 3,000 m, above the bamboo zone and into the Hagenia-Hypericum woodland belt. These bandas are a standard second-night stop for parties ascending the Chogoria route over three to four days. The surrounding woodland at this elevation contains large Hagenia trees draped in lichen, Hypericum shrubs, and the endemic giant lobelia (Lobelia deckenii) reaching two metres in height.

## 9. Rutundu Log Cabin, Northeast Slopes

Rutundu Log Cabin is a private luxury bush lodge at around 3,100 m on the northeastern slopes of Mount Kenya, accessible by four-wheel drive from Nanyuki. The cabin is famous as the place where Prince William proposed to Kate Middleton in October 2010. The log cabin style — dark timber, stone fireplace, antelope trophies from the colonial era — is a distinctly different atmosphere from the KWS bandas and represents the premium Kenya bush-lodge experience transposed to a high-altitude setting. Trout fishing in the Rutundu Lake is a key activity.

## 10. Guy Yeoman Hut, Chogoria Route

Guy Yeoman Hut is a small shelter on the Chogoria route in the upper moorland zone below the Hall Tarns. The hut provides emergency shelter and basic overnight accommodation for parties ascending or descending the eastern route, and is maintained by the Mountain Club of Kenya, founded in 1924 and one of the oldest mountaineering clubs in Africa. The surrounding moorland at the Guy Yeoman level is carpeted with everlastings (Helichrysum) and giant groundsels in July and August.
