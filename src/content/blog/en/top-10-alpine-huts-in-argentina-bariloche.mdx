San Carlos de Bariloche sits on the southern shore of Lago Nahuel Huapi at around 800 metres above sea level, ringed by a dramatic Andean landscape of glacially carved lakes, beech forests, and jagged peaks that reach above 3,400 metres. Nahuel Huapi National Park, established in 1934 and Argentina's oldest, protects nearly 710,000 hectares of this terrain, making it the centrepiece of the Argentine Lake District. The backcountry hut network here is modest compared to the Alps but covers the main trekking circuits with staffed refugios that offer beds, meals, and logistical support for multi-day routes. The main hiking season runs from November through April; snow closes higher routes from May. Argentine refugios typically charge per person for beds and meals separately, and the most popular ones — especially near Cerro Catedral — fill up rapidly in January and February, the peak Southern Hemisphere summer weeks.

## 1. Refugio San Martín / Refugio Jakob, Laguna Jakob

Refugio Jakob, also called Refugio San Martín, sits at 1,630 metres above Laguna Jakob in a glacial cirque northeast of Bariloche. The approach from the Villa Catedral ski base takes five to six hours through lenga beech forest and open rock slabs. The refugio was built by the Centro Andino Bariloche in the 1930s and modernised several times since; it currently sleeps around 60 in bunks across several rooms. A full dinner service features pasta and stews. The position makes it a natural hub for the four-day Laguna Jakob Circuit and for acclimatisation walks toward Cerro Bastión and the Argentine-Chilean border ridge.

## 2. Refugio Emilio Frey, Laguna Toncek

Refugio Frey occupies one of the most spectacular settings in Patagonian mountain architecture: a stone building perched at 1,700 metres beside the intensely turquoise Laguna Toncek, with towering granite spires — the Torres del Frey — rising behind. Established in 1933 and named after Emilio Frey, an early surveyor of the Argentine Andes, it is the most photographed refugio in the Lake District. The approach from Villa Catedral takes three to four hours. Frey is a major destination for rock climbers; the granite walls are graded up to 6c and longer multi-pitch routes top out above 2,000 metres. Beds for 80 people book out weeks ahead in January.

## 3. Refugio Italia Manfredo Segre, Laguna Negra

Named after the Italian Alpine Club section that funded its construction and the Italian mountaineer Manfredo Segre, this refugio at 1,850 metres above Laguna Negra is the highest-altitude staffed shelter in the immediate Bariloche hinterland. The route from Frey adds another four to five hours of increasingly exposed terrain. The building sleeps 30 and operates a small kitchen through December to April. Experienced mountaineers use it as a base for routes on Cerro Catedral Norte and the rarely climbed ridgelines along the watershed with Chile. Snow lingers in the basin until late November and returns in April.

## 4. Refugio Agostino Rocca, Cerro Catedral

Refugio Rocca sits at the base of Cerro Catedral's ski lifts at 1,030 metres, making it the most accessible overnight option in the network. The building serves primarily as a ski-season lodge but operates year-round for walkers using the lift system as an approach shortcut into higher terrain. Accommodation runs to around 40 beds in basic rooms; the restaurant is well-stocked by mountain standards. It is named after Agostino Rocca, an Italian industrialist who invested heavily in the Bariloche ski infrastructure during the mid-twentieth century.

## 5. Refugio Hielo Azul, Cerro Hielo Azul

Located in the Valle del Hielo Azul roughly 75 kilometres southwest of Bariloche, near the town of El Bolsón, Refugio Hielo Azul sits at around 1,400 metres below the namesake blue-ice glacier. The three-to-four-hour approach from the trailhead at Los Repollos winds through ancient arrayán myrtle forest. The refugio is a private operation with 30 bunk beds, a wood-fired kitchen, and a wood-burning hot tub that justifies the visit even on cold evenings. The glacier itself retreated noticeably during the 1990s and 2000s but remains a compelling destination.

## 6. Refugio Cajón de Azul, Río Azul gorge

Refugio Cajón de Azul stands in a narrow gorge of the Río Azul at around 700 metres, southeast of El Bolsón. The access requires a four-hour walk through dense coihue beech forest, including several river crossings that become difficult in spring snowmelt. The refugio is a timber construction with 30 beds and a kitchen that relies on the river for water and a micro-hydro system for lighting. The gorge walls rise nearly 400 metres above the shelter and create a microclimate with unusual humidity and abundant mosses. Day-trippers and overnight walkers share the space without the extreme booking pressure found closer to Bariloche.

## 7. Refugio Natación, Laguna Natación

Refugio Natación sits beside the lake of the same name at approximately 1,550 metres, accessible from Villa Catedral in about four hours via a trail branching off the main Frey route. It is a smaller, quieter alternative to Frey, sleeping around 25, with a simple cooking area. The name refers to the unusually warm swimming temperatures of the lake in summer — a product of shallow depth and good southern exposure. Climbers use the hut as a base for lesser-frequented rock routes on the walls surrounding the lake.

## 8. Refugio Gonzalez, Nahuel Huapi backcountry

Refugio Gonzalez operates in a remote valley within Nahuel Huapi National Park, well east of the main Catedral circuit. Reached via a long approach from the park's northern road access points, it sees far less traffic than the Bariloche-adjacent huts and offers solitude in the lenga beech and ñire forests that define the drier eastern edge of the park. Capacity is modest — around 20 beds — and the kitchen operates with limited provisions; walkers are advised to carry supplementary food for longer stays.

## 9. Refugio López, Cerro López

Refugio López at 1,620 metres on the flanks of Cerro López is among the oldest continuously operating mountain shelters in Argentine Patagonia, originally established in the late 1930s. The ascent from the Llao Llao hotel area takes three to four hours through open moorland, and the views over Lago Nahuel Huapi from the ridge above the hut are among the most photographed in the Lake District. It sleeps 50 and has a well-regarded kitchen. A further two-hour climb gains the summit of Cerro López at 2,076 metres, one of the most accessible real alpine summits from Bariloche.

## 10. Refugio Berghof, Villa Catedral base

Refugio Berghof operates at the Villa Catedral ski base area and caters to both skiers in winter and trekkers in summer. The German-influenced name reflects the European immigrant community that developed Bariloche's outdoor culture from the 1930s onward. The building has hotel-standard rooms as well as dormitory beds and a full restaurant; it functions as a trailhead staging point for the entire Catedral circuit. Lift passes sold here can shorten the approach to Frey or Jakob by 300 metres of vertical gain, making it a pragmatic choice for walkers with limited acclimatisation time.
