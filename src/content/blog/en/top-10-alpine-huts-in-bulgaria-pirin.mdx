The Pirin Mountains of southwestern Bulgaria form one of the most dramatic highland landscapes in the Balkans. Stretching roughly 80 kilometres from Predela Pass in the north to the Struma valley in the south, Pirin reaches its apex at Vihren (2,914 m), the country's third-highest summit. The range was inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List in 1983, recognised for its glacial lakes, old-growth Macedonian pine forests, and biodiversity that includes species found nowhere else in Bulgaria. The Bulgarian mountain shelter — the хижа (hizha) — has been the backbone of hiking culture here for over a century, with most established in the early to mid-twentieth century by mountaineering clubs based in Sofia, Plovdiv, and Blagoevgrad. The network today allows multi-day traverses through the highest massifs without carrying camping gear, and the ten huts below represent the essential staging posts for anyone serious about exploring Pirin.

## 1. Хижа Демяница, Northern Pirin

Хижа Демяница sits at approximately 1,895 m in the beech and pine transition zone on the northern slopes of Pirin, reached by a two-hour trail from the village of Bansko or from the Vihren chairlift terminus. The hut sleeps around 90 guests in dormitory and private rooms and offers a full kitchen service with hearty Bulgarian mountain fare — banitsa, grilled meats, bean soup. It functions as a base for ascents of Vihren via the northern cirque and serves day-trippers from the nearby ski resort throughout winter. The hut operates year-round with reduced winter staff.

## 2. Хижа Синаница, Northern Pirin

Хижа Синаница occupies a forest clearing at roughly 1,980 m, northeast of the Vihren peak group. The approach from Bansko follows the Demyanitsa river valley for about 12 km. The hut has a traditional log-and-stone construction with capacity for around 40 people and is particularly popular in July and August when the surrounding meadows are in full bloom. From Sinanitsa, trails lead east toward the Sinanitsa Lake and onward to the Koncheto ridge — the most technically demanding section of the main Pirin traverse.

## 3. Хижа Яворов, Central Pirin

Named after the Bulgarian poet and revolutionary Peyo Yavorov, хижа Яворов stands at 2,070 m at the foot of the Vihren cirque, making it one of the highest-positioned huts in northern Pirin. It sleeps approximately 100 people and is one of the busiest huts in the range, positioned directly on the route toward Vihren summit via the standard south-ridge trail. The hut has a large dining room and was renovated in the 2000s. The season runs from late May through early October, though the structure is locked in deep winter.

## 4. Хижа Безбог, Northern Pirin

Хижа Безбог at 2,235 m overlooks the Bezbozhki Lakes group — a chain of five glacial lakes arranged in a staircase below the rocky crests of Polezhan and Bayuvi Dupki. The hut is accessible via a chairlift from the village of Dobrinishte, which makes it unusually easy to reach for its altitude. The terrace faces west, offering sunset views across the lakes toward Alibotush massif. Accommodation capacity is around 80 beds. The chairlift season is typically June to September.

## 5. Хижа Бъндерица, Northern Pirin

Sitting at 1,770 m in the Banderitsa river valley, хижа Бъндерица is one of the oldest huts in the range, established in the 1920s by the Pirin mountaineering society. It offers dormitory-style accommodation for around 60 people and serves as a lower base camp for the Vihren approach. The forest around the hut contains several protected Macedonian pines over 1,000 years old — one of the key elements of the UNESCO designation. A short trail from the hut leads to the Banderitsa Lakes.

## 6. Хижа Кончето, Central Pirin

Хижа Кончето is positioned at 2,447 m near the namesake ridge — a narrow, exposed rock arête that is the most iconic crossing in Bulgarian hiking. The "Koncheto" (little horse) ridge runs barely a metre wide along the crest for roughly 400 metres, with chains and fixed handholds on its steepest sections. The hut provides accommodation for around 30 people and emergency shelter for those caught by afternoon thunderstorms — a common hazard on the exposed central massif. Access from either Sinanitsa or Tevno Ezero takes three to four hours.

## 7. Хижа Тевно Езеро, Central Pirin

Хижа Тевно Езеро — named for the small, dark-watered Tevno Lake at 2,512 m — is the highest permanently staffed hut in Pirin. It sits in a treeless glacial bowl surrounded by the peaks of Kutelo, Polezhan, and Bezbog, and the isolation is complete: the nearest trailhead is at least five hours' walk. Capacity is modest at around 40 beds. The lake itself, permanently cold and clear, reflects the surrounding crests. This hut marks the midpoint of the classic two- to three-day northern Pirin traverse.

## 8. Хижа Полежан, Southern Pirin

Хижа Полежан stands at 2,010 m on the southern flanks of Polezhan peak (2,851 m), in the quieter, less-visited middle sector of the range. It has capacity for around 50 people and offers a markedly different atmosphere from the crowded northern huts — fewer tourists, longer approach trails from the town of Gotse Delchev, and a wilder surrounding landscape with good chances of spotting chamois at dawn. The hut is the main base for the traverse of the southern high ridge.

## 9. Хижа Пирин, Northern Pirin

Хижа Пирин at 1,760 m is located in the Vihren National Park near the entry checkpoint for the protected core zone. It is typically the last comfortable overnight stop before ascending into the higher elevations, and its large dining room and 80-bed capacity make it a good first-night base for parties arriving from Sofia or Blagoevgrad by bus. The hut has been operating for nearly 90 years in various forms and remains one of the most recognisable names in the Pirin network.

## 10. Хижа Вихрен, Northern Pirin

Хижа Вихрен at 1,950 m sits below the north face of the mountain it is named for, on the main trail connecting the Bansko trailhead to the Vihren summit plateau. It is typically open from May through October and sleeps around 60 people. The final ascent from this hut to the Vihren summit (2,914 m) takes approximately two hours by the standard route along the south ridge, or longer via the more exposed northwest arête. In clear conditions the summit offers views to the Rhodopes, the Rila, and on exceptional days, the Aegean coast.
