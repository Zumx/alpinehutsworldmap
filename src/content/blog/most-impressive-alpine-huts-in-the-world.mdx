Some huts are shelter. A few are architecture, engineering, and mountaineering
history compressed into one improbable building. These are the alpine huts that
make people stop and stare before they even open the door.

## Monte Rosa Hut, Switzerland

A five-storey faceted aluminium crystal on the glacier below the Dufourspitze.
It runs largely on solar power and recycled meltwater, and it is widely cited
as the most advanced self-sufficient building in the Alps. Reaching it involves
a glacier crossing — this is a mountaineer's hut, not a stroll.

## Refuge du Goûter, France

The launch point for Mont Blanc by the normal route, rebuilt as an ovoid
stainless-steel pod bolted to a ridge at 3,835 m. Anchored against ferocious
wind, it looks more like a spacecraft than a shelter.

## Solvayhütte, Switzerland

An emergency bivouac clinging to the Hörnli ridge of the Matterhorn at 4,003 m.
You cannot book it; it exists to save lives when weather traps climbers on the
route. Few structures on Earth occupy a more dramatic perch.

## Capanna Margherita, Italy

The highest building in Europe, at 4,554 m on the summit of the Signalkuppe in
the Monte Rosa massif. It doubles as a high-altitude research laboratory.
Sleeping here means a night well into the zone where the air no longer
cooperates.

## Rifugio Lagazuoi, Italy

Not the most technical, but perhaps the most beloved view in the Dolomites: a
terrace at 2,752 m facing a sea of pale limestone towers turning gold at sunset,
with First World War tunnels in the rock beneath your feet.

## Berliner Hütte, Austria

A protected historic monument from the golden age of alpine clubs, with a
panelled, chandeliered dining hall that turns a remote Zillertal valley into an
unexpected piece of nineteenth-century grandeur.

## Tracuit Hut, Switzerland

A sharp modern wedge of timber and metal at 3,256 m, oriented to soak winter
sun and frame the Weisshorn. A textbook example of how new hut design treats
energy and view as the same problem.

## Gervasutti Hut, Italy

A cantilevered tube projecting off a rock spur in the Mont Blanc massif,
prefabricated in the valley and flown in by helicopter. It looks like it should
not be possible, which is precisely the point.

## What "impressive" really means up high

The huts that endure are not the flashiest — they are the ones that solve the
real problems: water without a mains supply, power without a grid, warmth
without a road for fuel, and structure against wind that would flatten a normal
building. Modern alpine huts are, quietly, some of the most demanding
small-building engineering anywhere.

## Why they matter beyond the view

These buildings are also climate instruments. Huts that once sat beside ice now
look down on bare rock; wardens keep some of the longest informal records of
glacier retreat in existence. Visiting them is, increasingly, witnessing change
in real time.
